## Summary

Andy Wood criticized Fender's trademark litigation against other guitar manufacturers over the Stratocaster body shape, arguing that even if Fender wins legally, they've already lost in public perception. If Fender prevails, the consequences would be severe: smaller manufacturers would face licensing fees of €250,000+ or be forced to cease production, custom builders would go out of business, and S-shaped guitars from Suhr, PRS (Silver Sky), Schecter, and others would have to be discontinued. The precedent could lead Fender to target other patented body shapes—Telecaster, Jazzmaster, offset designs—creating a monopoly that stifles industry innovation. The legal action reveals Fender's insecurity about competing on actual product quality and innovation.

For guitar buyers, the immediate actionable takeaway is to support competing brands like PRS, Suhr, Tom Anderson, Schecter, and Music Man Sabre, which make excellent instruments and now benefit from this litigation as a marketing advantage. You don't need to stop playing or stop buying existing Fender guitars—the used market offers 70 years of available instruments. The broader message is that the guitar industry thrives on competition; Fender's attempt to eliminate rivals through legal action rather than superior manufacturing makes them less attractive, and the company should instead respond by either withdrawing the lawsuit or actually improving their product quality to compete on merit.
