## Summary

The sous chef at Japanese restaurant shares her day managing prep work for seasonal fine dining. Starting at 8 AM with a daily menu meeting, she oversees prep cooks preparing dishes like braised lamb with red flint polenta, broccoli, and olive salsa verde, plus a citrus beurre blanc with petrale sole—all built around whatever fresh, local ingredients the farmers brought that week. She demonstrates key techniques: reducing garlic waste by using peels, blanching broccoli to preserve texture for line cooks, hand-chopping olives and capers with precision (rejecting machines), meticulously butchering fish to exact portions, and composting vegetable scraps. Her role is orchestrating timing so all prep finishes by 11:45 AM, loading components onto a dumb waiter by 2 PM, checking in with line cooks at 3 PM, and getting chef approval at 4 PM tasters before service begins at 5 PM.

To apply this approach: visit farmers markets and build relationships with specific farmers, then plan meals around what they actually have available that week rather than shopping a fixed grocery list. In your kitchen, start with the highest quality ingredients you can find—this eliminates the need for elaborate techniques to hide mediocre raw materials. Hand-prepare components (chop, blanch, portion) rather than relying on food processors or pre-made elements; this gives you precision and forces you to notice quality issues. Reduce waste by composting scraps and using all edible parts (herb stems, vegetable trimmings). Build your knife skills intentionally, and develop a routine that allows you to prep ahead so you're not rushed during actual cooking.
