## Summary

Vlad responds to a Russian civilian confused and horrified by drone attacks on Moscow, unable to comprehend why Ukraine would target Russian territory. Vlad analyzes the man's shock as extreme depoliticization: he cannot connect his government's four-year aggression against Ukraine (since 2014) to the blowback he's experiencing, cannot see himself as implicated in the Kremlin's actions, and treats the state as a separate entity from which he bears no responsibility. Vlad traces this psychological disconnect to authoritarianism, passivity, loss of agency, and a kind of post-modern relativism where causality becomes unknowable. However, Vlad then pivots to argue this is not uniquely Russian pathology—Western audiences suffer parallel depoliticization through consuming politics as entertainment, inability to hold complex contradictions in mind simultaneously, obsession with identity politics, and algorithmic deformation of thought.

Stop consuming politics as soap opera and recognize when you're depoliticizing: when you relate to political content through symbols and algorithmic triggers rather than understanding material causes; when you cannot simultaneously hold that something is both evil AND structurally persistent; when you use identity obsession as a substitute for understanding actual power dynamics; or when you're a centrist technocrat believing small technical fixes address a crisis of trust without engaging real public sentiment. The man in the video is an extreme version of what you're already doing—the actionable lesson is to notice your own depoliticization and build actual political comprehension rather than algorithmic reflexivity.
