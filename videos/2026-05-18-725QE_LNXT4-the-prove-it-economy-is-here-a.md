## Summary

**What Was Discussed:**
The internet is shifting from an "attention economy" to an "interpretation economy" where AI agents—not humans—filter most purchasing and hiring decisions. When people shop for products or search for candidates, they increasingly ask AI assistants (like Claude or ChatGPT) to find and evaluate options rather than browsing directly. This means success now depends on being "agent-legible": having clear, structured, factual data about what you offer that AI can reliably read and understand. The speaker uses running shoes as an example—instead of vague emotional marketing, companies need specific data about materials, spring systems, and energy savings that agents can parse. The same applies to job candidates, who must prove their skills with concrete evidence rather than claims. Both human brand loyalty and agent-legibility now matter equally; trying to game the system with "AI washing" (falsely claiming AI capabilities) backfires when agents discover the truth.

**What's Actionable:**
For marketers: Audit your website to ensure it contains structured, detailed, provable data about products that AI agents can extract. Build influence over product positioning, pricing, claims, and technical details—not just content creation. Develop a dual strategy: create memorable human experiences (especially offline events and networking) while structuring your data for agent interpretation. For job candidates: Build a portfolio demonstrating actual AI skills with concrete projects and proof, not resume claims. Show what you can build and do. Develop memorable relationships through offline networking while ensuring your online presence clearly explains your specific capabilities at a level of detail agents can understand. Have strong opinions and showcase specific expertise rather than trying to appeal to everyone—agents need differentiation to elevate you from noise.
