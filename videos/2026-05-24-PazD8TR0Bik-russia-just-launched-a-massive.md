## Summary

Russia launched a massive combined drone and missile attack on Ukraine on May 24th, striking mainly Kyiv with 690 total munitions (90 missiles and 600 drones), resulting in at least 4 deaths and over 100 wounded. Ukraine intercepted approximately 60% of the missiles and 90% of the drones, but the lower missile interception rate leaves significant damage and highlights Ukraine's critical shortage of ballistic missile defense systems. Russia claims the attack was retaliation for Ukraine allegedly striking a school in Russian-occupied Luhansk, but details remain disputed—evidence suggests the site may have been a military facility, and Ukraine's recent verified strikes have targeted only military objectives. The attack included the use of Orshnik intermediate-range ballistic missiles (nuclear-capable) for the third time, which the EU condemned as nuclear brinkmanship.

Viewers can support Ukrainian forces by donating to the Ukraine Aid Operations fundraiser (linked in the video description), raising $35,000 for ground drones actively used for ammunition resupply and casualty evacuation. Donation tiers range from $200 for a unit patch to $4,000 for a metal figurine set made from bullet casings, with all donations fully tax-deductible. A Ukrainian soldier from the 9th Unmanned Systems Brigade demonstrated in the video that these drones directly save lives on the front lines, making this a concrete way to fund an identified military need.
