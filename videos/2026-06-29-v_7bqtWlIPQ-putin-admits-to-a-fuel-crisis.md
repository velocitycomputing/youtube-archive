## Summary

Putin admitted at an emergency meeting that Russia faces fuel shortages caused by Ukrainian drone strikes on energy infrastructure, though he downplayed the severity. He acknowledged queues at gas stations and unavailable fuel grades while simultaneously blaming Western elites for "unprecedented pressure" aimed at destabilizing Russia. Security expert Ivana Stradner analyzed that this admission signals genuine weakness: Russia's economy depends on energy exports to fund its war, battlefield losses have demoralized Russians (who expected a 72-day conflict, not years), and Putin is now negotiating from desperation rather than strength. Ukraine's intensifying drone strikes deep into Russian territory demonstrate momentum, while China and India—Russia's main alternative partners—are unwilling to support a losing position.

The window for Western action is now. Stradner urges the EU, UK, and US to impose aggressive sanctions on Russia's energy sector to destabilize Putin's core power base, deliver all weapons Ukraine needs to maintain pressure, and reject any concessions at the negotiation table. Do not treat Putin's threats as credible deterrents. Target Ukraine's strikes at Russia's fuel and energy infrastructure to directly starve the war machine of funding, and recognize that Putin may attempt to "pause and reposition" if given breathing room—continued pressure prevents this escape route.
