Rick Bato critiques the New York Times' list of 30 greatest living American songwriters by reviewing the 30 included artists (Nile Rogers, Stevie Wonder, Taylor Swift, Bob Dylan, Bad Bunny, and others) before systematically detailing major omissions he believes should have made the cut—including Billy Joel, James Taylor, Jackson Browne, Eminem, John Fogerty, and modern hitmakers like Ryan Tedder (13+ billion-stream songs across multiple artists) and Ashley Gorley (83 number-one country songs).
