## Summary

A new German carnivore diet study tracked 24 people (average 17 months on the diet) using pre- and post-diet blood work. Of 22 participants with chronic health conditions, 17 improved or resolved their condition while zero worsened. Objective blood markers showed striking improvements: HbA1c dropped in pre-diabetic participants, all six participants with elevated triglycerides saw them decrease, and C-reactive protein (systemic inflammation marker) dropped from 0.6 to 0.1 mg/dL. Self-reported outcomes included 89% reporting improved energy, 69% improved strength/endurance, and widespread improvements in mental clarity, weight loss, and food cravings. Dr. Westman acknowledged this as "the best carnivore diet study to date" due to its long-term follow-up and objective blood work, while noting limitations: small sample size, self-reported diet adherence, no control group, and selection bias toward people doing well.

If you have chronic metabolic issues or prediabetes, the evidence suggests trying carnivore may improve blood markers and symptom resolution, with improvements typically visible after the initial adaptation period (beyond 2 weeks). If you have gout, restrict meat and purines initially while your metabolic health recovers, then you may tolerate them later as insulin levels normalize. Commit fully to the diet without cheating—cravings typically disappear within days. Be aware this study has meaningful limitations, and more rigorous prospective trials are needed to determine how broadly these results apply, particularly regarding dropout rates and long-term adherence in the general population.
