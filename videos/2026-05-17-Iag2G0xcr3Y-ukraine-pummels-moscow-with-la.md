## Summary

Ukraine conducted its largest drone attack on Moscow on May 17, 2026, deploying approximately 310 drones and 8 unmanned surface vehicles targeting critical infrastructure. Major strikes hit the Solnechnogorskaya oil pumping station (a Transneft pipeline node), oil depots, the Zelenograd microelectronics factory (US-sanctioned), the Raduga missile development plant, Sheremetyevo Airport, and Black Sea Fleet targets in Crimea including Belbek airfield and communications hubs. Despite Russian claims of destroying 556 drones, Ukraine's 97% intercept rate on Russian counterstrikes and documented fires across multiple facilities confirm significant damage. Three Russians died and 16+ were injured; additionally, the Russian resistance group Black Spark claimed to have destroyed Gazprom pipelines near St. Petersburg.

The video indicates that Russian air defenses—historically Moscow's primary shield—failed to contain the assault, undermining Russia's defensive mythology. For viewers tracking the conflict, this demonstrates Ukraine's expanding precision strike capability deep into Russian territory and Russia's inability to protect even heavily fortified zones. Concrete developments to monitor: (1) the reimposition of US sanctions on Russian seaborne oil following expiration of Trump's temporary waiver, which tightens economic pressure, and (2) whether Russia will reinforce air defenses or redirect forces in response, affecting battlefield dynamics elsewhere.
