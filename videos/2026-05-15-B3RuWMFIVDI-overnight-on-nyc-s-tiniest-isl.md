## Summary

The creator paddled to Steinway Island, New York City's smallest island in the East River, which was formed in the 1890s from granite debris excavated during tunnel construction between Manhattan and Queens. Now home to a large cormorant colony, the island offered spectacular views of the UN Headquarters, Chrysler Building, Empire State Building, and One World Trade Center. After battling strong winds and tides to reach the island, he camped overnight under an archway, ate cold New York pizza from Williamsburg, and slept well despite the rocky terrain and bird neighbors.

To replicate this trip: launch a kayak from Brooklyn and paddle east toward the East River during slack tide or with tailwinds if possible; plan for a 1.5-2 mile journey against potentially fierce currents. Arrive early afternoon to set up camp (bring a cot for uneven ground and hand sanitizer). Time your departure for early morning (around 5:30-5:45 AM) to catch the outgoing tide for an easier return paddle. Confirm no active bird nests before camping, and be prepared for the pungent smell and occasional animal remains on the island.
