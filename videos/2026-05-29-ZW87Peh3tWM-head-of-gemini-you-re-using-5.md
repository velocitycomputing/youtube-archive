## Summary

Google's Head of Gemini Josh Woodward discussed the company's shift into the agentic era, particularly Gemini Spark—a 24/7 AI agent rolling out this week to testers and next week to Pro users. He highlighted new capabilities including voice features that intelligently pull from Gmail and Drive to create documents, the ability to run hundreds of parallel tasks using Google Cloud VMs, and a generative media suite (images, videos, songs) that competitors lack. Woodward emphasized the fundamental shift from "doing" to "directing"—where knowledge workers orchestrate AI agents rather than executing tasks manually. He also discussed Notebook LM for assembling context, moving toward voice-first interfaces, and how personal intelligence deeply integrates Google's ecosystem without requiring separate connectors.

To immediately benefit: enable Gemini Spark when available and try the specific prompt "What three meetings should I cancel this week?" to free up calendar time. Create an MD file documenting your personal principles and upload it to Gemini so it learns your tone and decision-making style. Build a personal notebook by syncing your best newsletters, book highlights (via Readwise), and notes—Gemini will reference these automatically. Try the voice feature when it launches for hands-free context retrieval. Most importantly, spend weekly time experimenting with Gemini to build intuition about its strengths and limitations, treating it as a strategic partner rather than a one-off tool.
