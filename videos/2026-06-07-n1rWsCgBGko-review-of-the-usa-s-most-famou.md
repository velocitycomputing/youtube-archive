## Summary

The reviewer visited The French Laundry in Yountville, California—one of America's most famous 3 Michelin-star restaurants—and documented a multi-course tasting menu spanning appetizers like smoked salmon mousse cones, Japanese seafood preparations, and the signature oysters and pearls through mains including king crab, beef wellington, and even mac and cheese with black truffle. Each course was paired with premium wines (Krug champagne, Japanese sake, 1954 Madeira, burgundies) and served in the restaurant's elegant white-washed dining room, originally a 19th-century French steam laundry. The total bill came to approximately €1,500, and the reviewer praised the nearly flawless execution, exceptional service (particularly highlighting server Brighton), and innovative cuisine by Chef de Cuisine David Breeden—rating the experience as vastly superior to Thomas Keller's Per Se in New York.

**Actionable takeaway:** Getting a reservation at The French Laundry is extremely difficult and requires insider connections (the reviewer only secured a table through an operations director he knew in Singapore). If you want to attempt dining here, leverage hospitality industry contacts who may have relationships with the restaurant. The €1,500+ investment is worthwhile only if you can confirm a reservation beforehand, and the reviewer recommends visiting California rather than Keller's New York location, as his entire California Michelin dining tour yielded four exceptional restaurants in close proximity—a rarity globally.
