## Summary

Loy and Lewis is Austin's first Michelin-starred barbecue restaurant, opened in 2024 after seven years operating as a food truck. The operation exemplifies "new school barbecue"—traditional smoking techniques (using post oak, careful temperature control, detailed logging) combined with expanded menus beyond standard cuts. The restaurant smokes brisket, chicken, flat iron, and beef cheeks over 12–15 hours, grinds roughly 200 burgers daily, and sources all meat from local Texas suppliers. The menu spans signature dishes like the LNL burger (double-ground smoked brisket with mayo-barbecue sauce), pulled whole hog, beef cheeks, and sides like cholo papas and Texas red chili Frito pie, with desserts including cheddar cheesecake and German chocolate cobbler. Service is counter-casual in the main dining room (order at the barbecue line), with a bar and additional dining rooms, a weekend dinner reservation menu featuring hand-cut house steaks, and an "Anytime Eats" menu for customers unwilling to wait.

If visiting, plan for roughly a one-hour wait during peak hours (open Wed–Mon, 11am–9pm), but you can order drinks from the bar while waiting. Order the LNL burger and beef cheeks (the pit master's favorite), and try the Texas red chili Frito pie if you want something unique. Skip the wait by choosing the Anytime Eats menu, booking a weekend dinner reservation (no line, full table service with smoked steak options), or visiting the Saturday bacon rib special.
