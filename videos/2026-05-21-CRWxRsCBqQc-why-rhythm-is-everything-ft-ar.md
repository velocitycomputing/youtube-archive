## Summary

Ariel Posen demonstrates how to transcend scale-based playing by grounding guitar work in rhythm and groove over a simple kick-snare beat. He covers using minimal chord progressions (E, G, A), laying back behind the beat to create tension like J Dilla, adding ghost notes to function as your own rhythm section, and mastering chord inversions and voicings across the fretboard using triads. He emphasizes always keeping the underlying chords mentally active while soloing, thinking in key centers rather than modes, and treating every passage as part of a coherent song rather than mindless noodling.

Practice playing over the provided kick-snare beat starting with three simple chords and focus exclusively on rhythm and timing before adding complexity. Systematically memorize where each chord (E, G, A) appears in different inversions across every string and position on the neck—Posen stresses this takes 95% memorization and 5% talent. Learn the difference between straight, swung, and slightly-behind-the-beat ("Dilla") feels, then deliberately practice each until you can shift between them. Record yourself soloing while mentally keeping the bass notes and chord changes present, even when not playing them directly.
