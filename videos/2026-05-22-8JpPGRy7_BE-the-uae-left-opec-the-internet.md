## Summary

The video explains that OPEC was founded in 1960 to stabilize oil prices during the post-WWII era when Western companies kept prices artificially low. The UAE is leaving due to tensions with Saudi Arabia over production quotas that disadvantage ADOC (the UAE's national oil company) and their opposing stances in Sudan and Libya conflicts. While the internet claims this is world-changing, OPEC membership has historically been dynamic, with countries regularly joining and leaving.

Despite viral panic, the presenter argues the UAE's departure won't significantly impact oil prices—member departures are precedent-setting and the UAE is a minor player. The outcome may be increased competition forcing more oil onto the market, but the Strait of Hormuz situation could offset any price decreases. Focus on actual energy fundamentals rather than internet sensationalism about OPEC's stability.
