## Summary

The video identifies 90% of knee problems as stemming from restricted rotational movement: the tibia (shin bone) becomes stuck in external rotation while the femur (thigh bone) locks in internal rotation, reducing joint space and causing injuries like knee valgus, ACL tears, and arthritis. This restriction originates from tight muscles (inner thigh and calf) and limited mobility at the hip and foot, which prevent the knee from rotating in the opposite direction it needs to move. The solution involves teaching the knee to rotate the other way by addressing these upstream restrictions and movement patterns.

To fix your knee, follow this progression: (1) foam roll your inner thigh and outer calf for 60 seconds each to release tight muscles; (2) perform half-kneeling shin rotations (2 sets of 20 reps) to learn opposite rotation while staying heel-heavy; (3) do side-lying glute/hip work with leg swings (2-3 sets of 15 reps); (4) perform stagger squats on a foam roller against a wall (3 sets of 8-10 reps, staying heel-heavy); (5) do diagonal lunges (3 sets of 8-10 reps each side), focusing on proper foot pronation and preventing knee cave-in; and (6) finish with split squats (3 sets of 6-10 reps) with the back leg elevated, emphasizing controlled forward shin movement. Perform these exercises in sequence to both mobilize the knee and strengthen its new range of motion.
