## Summary

Jazmine, born in Japan to British parents, shares her journey of growing up as the only foreign child in a tiny village of eight houses, where she developed Japanese as her native language but experienced persistent microaggressions in school despite her fluency. She recounts struggling to navigate her identity between two cultures, excelling in Japanese language class to prove her belonging, and discovering transformative confidence at age 15 during a two-week trip to Italy—where seeing taller women and buying properly-fitting clothes shifted her self-perception. After university in Fukui where she finally met English speakers her age, she now works in Japan and is launching "Sachi-Coastal," a brand promoting sustainability, inclusivity, and the message that differences should be celebrated rather than hidden.

If you're a third-culture kid or foreigner navigating Japan, actively seek out others in similar situations through communities and social networks—isolation amplifies the struggle. Consider documenting and sharing your story through interviews, social media, or a personal project; Jazmine emphasizes that visibility helps isolated people realize they're not alone. Reframe your differences as strengths: pursue what makes you unique rather than conforming, and remember that locals' microaggressions typically stem from unfamiliarity, not malice—respond with openness rather than retreating.
