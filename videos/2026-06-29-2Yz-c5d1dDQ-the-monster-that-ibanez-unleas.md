## Summary

The speaker discovered Ibanez Prestige guitars through watching Polyphia and modern neo-soul players, shifting his perception from seeing Ibanez as metal instruments to versatile modern alternatives. He explores the A22040N model's distinctive features: contemporary construction with side-mounted output jack and snap-in heel socket, 22 frets on a 25.5" scale, and crucially, an "ultra switch" that reconfigures pickups across five positions to deliver humbucker-like tones, Telecaster-style sounds, and various hybrid configurations. Beyond the switching versatility, he emphasizes consistent quality in fretwork, hardware, and overall build as key reasons these guitars have gained traction among modern players.

If you play Strat-style guitars and want a broader range of tones without switching instruments, the Ibanez Prestige line—particularly the A22040N—offers a viable alternative to traditional Fenders. The ultra switch gives you multiple pickup configurations to experiment with across different genres, and the quality construction ensures reliable fretwork and hardware. Try one at a local dealer or through Sweetwater (featured in the video) to experience the difference firsthand.
