## Summary

The interview featured Justin (Pink's guitarist) discussing his 20-year partnership with Pink and 15-year role with The Voice band, his transition from analog rigs to the Fractal Audio digital modeler for all touring and recording, and his advanced playing techniques. He demonstrated alternate picking, hybrid picking, and sweep picking for speed—particularly his signature one-pick-per-string method learned from Rick Graham and Tom Quail. He covered his Les Paul preferences (balanced neck/bridge brightness, appropriate thickness, darker neck tones for lead work), his intense recording workflow at Henson Studios (14-hour days tracking 15+ songs with three passes each at roughly 30 minutes per song), and his new Shabbat signature guitar featuring custom Seymour Duncan pickups engineered through direct A/B testing.

To apply this to your own playing: master alternate picking with consistent downstrokes, practice one-pick-per-string on string changes for fluidity, use hybrid picking with sweep strokes for complex arpeggio patterns, and apply palm muting near the bridge to create a legato attack sound. Build a modeler template with 5-6 core patches instead of chasing option paralysis with hundreds of settings. Use the neck pickup for higher-register leads (warmer tone), rotate between pickups during performances to vary color, and practice right-hand technique relentlessly despite inconsistency—Justin emphasizes this takes constant work. Take his online masterclass (link in description, 30-day guarantee) or watch his JTC videos for visual breakdowns of these techniques, and consider short-scale guitars if traditional scales feel awkward.
