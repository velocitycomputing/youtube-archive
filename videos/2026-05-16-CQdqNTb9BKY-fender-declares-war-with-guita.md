## Summary

Fender has sent cease-and-desist letters across Europe to small guitar builders and retailers, demanding they stop selling Stratocaster-shaped guitars, recall all inventory, destroy the instruments, and pay €250,000 plus €3,500 in legal fees (with €15,000 penalties per violation if they continue). The speaker argues this aggressive action—motivated by a soft guitar market—is anti-competitive and unfair, especially since the Strat body shape has been public domain in the US since 2009 and has become an ergonomic standard that fills customer needs Fender hasn't addressed. He emphasizes that competitors like Charvel, Jackson, and Ibanez created Strat-style guitars because customers wanted features Fender wasn't providing, and that the 70-year-old design is now utility-based, not purely artistic.

Small builders and retailers facing these letters should immediately contact each other to coordinate a response, as many have already been served. Legal defense will cost at least $5,000 in the US plus equivalent or higher fees for German representation—consider reaching out to the podcast host for connection support or exploring crowdfunding for legal fees. Retailers like Thomann should fight the claim collectively. If you manufacture or sell Strat-style guitars in Europe, document your position on whether this is a business utility rather than copyright infringement, and consult with international trade attorneys familiar with EU design law before complying or responding to any legal notice.
