## Summary

Fender updated the Tone Master Pro digital modeling unit and removed all brand references from its manual—previously crediting real amps like Marshall, Mesa Boogie, Ibanez, and others that inspired the digital models. This is unusual because the entire modeling industry has historically included these credits. The speaker explores two possible explanations: either a manufacturer legally pressured Fender to stop using their brand names, or Fender is preemptively clearing its own act before taking legal action against other modelers for using Fender designs without permission—attempting to claim ownership of iconic models like the Deluxe Reverb, Twin, and Princeton the way other companies guard their trademarks.

If you use digital amp modeling software, monitor whether other manufacturers begin removing brand references or changing model names—this could signal an IP crackdown that limits access to recognizable amp and pedal emulations you rely on. If you're considering purchasing a modeling unit, the Tone Master Pro's removal of attribution may preview a broader shift toward generic naming and less transparent modeling lineage. Document which real amps your current models are based on now, as future updates may obscure that information and make it harder to understand what you're actually emulating.
