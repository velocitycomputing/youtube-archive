## Summary

The instructor teaches George Harrison's iconic "Let It Be" solo by breaking it down into 15 distinct phrases, focusing on two "landmark shapes" of the pentatonic scale (shape 1 and shape 4). These shapes can be located by finding the root note C on the low E and A strings, and can be extended diagonally across the entire neck to enable soloing in any key. To apply this, locate the two landmark pentatonic shapes in C major, then practice extending them diagonally across your neck until they feel like one connected system.
