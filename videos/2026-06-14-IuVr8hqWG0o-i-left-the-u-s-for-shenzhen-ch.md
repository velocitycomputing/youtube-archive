## Summary

Bradley Cray, a 36-year-old American who moved to Shenzhen, China in 2025, shared his life as an expat raising a family there. Shenzhen offers stark contrasts between modernized skyscrapers and traditional villages, with exceptional public transportation (subway rides cost 60 cents), underground shopping malls, affordable childcare ($300/semester including food), and universal healthcare ($90/month for his family of four). He works as a marketing manager earning $3,500/month base salary plus $4,500+ from social media content, lives in a three-bedroom apartment for $1,000/month, buys groceries for $100 monthly, and benefits from 15-minute delivery services for almost anything. His wife is a stay-at-home mom from Tahiti, their children are becoming trilingual (English, French, and Mandarin), and he appreciates the reduced anxiety around school shootings and kidnappings compared to America.

For anyone considering this move: China is not an immigration country, so strict visa compliance is mandatory or deportation follows—this isn't a permanent relocation option without proper status. The cost of living is genuinely low (filling a grocery cart for $100, WiFi at $29/month), and employment opportunities exist through teaching English or remote/freelance work. You'll need basic Mandarin for daily life, must adapt to dense urban living with no personal space, and should expect hazards like aggressive scooter traffic. This move works best for those with family ties, remote income, or language teaching skills, and who view it as a temporary-to-medium-term experience rather than permanent settlement.
