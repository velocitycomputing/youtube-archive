## Summary

Over the past week, Ukraine significantly escalated operations by striking Russian-occupied territories—targeting logistics routes (the M14 highway), military training camps, command posts, and industrial facilities—culminating in a controversial May 22 strike on Starobilsk that Russia claims killed mostly teenage girls at a teacher training college, while Ukraine claims it targeted military infrastructure. Russia responded with an unprecedented assault: 90 missiles and 600 drones (including Oreshnik hypersonic missiles) on Kyiv and other cities on May 23. Ukraine immediately retaliated with strikes on Russian air bases, aircraft repair plants, oil refineries, and the Central Bank facility in Crimea. Both sides continue escalating with no resolution in sight.

For stakeholders, several actionable concerns emerge: residents and diplomats in Kyiv should prepare for imminent severe strikes on military and administrative districts (Russia explicitly warned of "systematic strikes"); Ukraine is urgently requesting accelerated U.S. anti-ballistic missile deliveries due to critical shortfalls; and international observers should monitor whether hardline Russian voices pushing nuclear escalation gain influence over Putin, as the conflict approaches a potential tactical nuclear threshold with no established diplomatic off-ramp.
