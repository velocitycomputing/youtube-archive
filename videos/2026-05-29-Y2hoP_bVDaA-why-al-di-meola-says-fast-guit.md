## Summary

Al Di Meola recounts his journey joining Return to Forever at 19—launched by a friend's LSD-fueled recording played to Chick Corea—and his evolution as a guitarist through the 1970s and beyond. He discusses developing hybrid picking and Latin-influenced right-hand technique, his collaborations with Paco de Lucía and John McLaughlin on the legendary 7-million-selling album, and how constant touring with total focus created unmatched interplay. Central to the conversation is his observation that speed alone doesn't make a great musician; what separates exceptional players is musicality, the ability to read charts and respond to harmonic changes, strong rhythmic foundation (especially Latin feels), and fully developed musical ideas—not just technical velocity.

For guitarists, the actionable takeaway is stark: eliminate phone and computer time to reclaim 5–7 hours daily for focused practice, prioritize learning to read music and understand harmony over chasing speed, develop your rhythmic sense through Latin music and paying attention to clave/upbeat feel, and seek musical interplay rather than solo technique. Study diverse composers (Bach, Piazzolla, flamenco) to expand your harmonic palette, write your own music to deepen your voice, and understand that sustained, undistracted practice during your formative years compounds in ways that can't be replicated later.
