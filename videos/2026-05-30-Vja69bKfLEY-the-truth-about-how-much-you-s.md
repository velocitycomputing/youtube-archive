## Summary

The discussion debunked the 10,000-step myth (which originated from a Japanese pedometer marketing campaign) and reframed walking as a physiological necessity comparable to breathing and sleep. Research shows that 5,000 steps daily significantly reduces depression symptoms, while benefits plateau around 9,500 steps with diminishing returns afterward. For those struggling with movement, a "microwalk" of just 5 minutes (~500 steps) serves as an accessible entry point. The experts emphasized specific walking mechanics: arm rotation, walking tall, soft footstrikes that graze the heel (not heavy landing), and varied terrain to train movement variability. They also highlighted that a 10-15 minute walk within 10 minutes after meals is equivalent to a 45-minute walk for blood sugar control, and discussed how walking at 130-135 steps per minute (brisk pace) delivers cardiovascular and cancer-prevention benefits.

Start by aiming for 5,000 steps daily if you're sedentary, or begin with 5-minute microwalks if that feels overwhelming. After each meal, take a 10-15 minute walk as soon as possible (ideally within 10 minutes) to manage blood glucose. Spend a few barefoot minutes indoors to reset your footstrike mechanics and identify if you're landing too heavily. Use a step-tracking wearable (Garmin, Whoop, or Morpheus) to monitor pace and ensure you're hitting 130-135 steps per minute for cardiovascular benefits. Prioritize arm swing and upright posture when walking, avoid relying solely on heavily cushioned shoes, and incorporate varied terrain like hills or uneven ground to build foot and ankle stability for longevity and injury prevention.
