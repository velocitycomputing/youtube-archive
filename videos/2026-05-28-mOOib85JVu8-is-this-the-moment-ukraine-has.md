## Summary

Ukrainian military leaders and Institute for the Study of War analysts argue the war is entering a critical new phase where Ukraine may finally break through Russian positional defenses. Key indicators of shifting momentum include: Russian rates of advance plummeting from 15 square kilometers daily to 2-3 daily; Ukrainian forces regaining territory for the first time since 2023; improved Ukrainian command systems enabling coordinated logistics strikes against Russian supply lines 100+ kilometers behind the front; successful destruction of Russian air defenses allowing deeper drone operations; and Russian casualties outpacing recruitment since December 2025. Experts emphasize this represents a genuine tactical shift driven by Ukrainian operational sophistication, not fantasy—though they caution this advantage is temporary and could reverse if Russia adapts.

The next six months are critical to exploit. Watch Ukraine's ability to disrupt Russian logistics and supply lines, particularly the Crimea-Rostov Highway supply corridor, as this directly determines whether Ukraine can sustain mechanized counterattacks and territorial gains. Monitor Russian casualty and mobilization rates as indicators of whether Moscow can sustain current operations. Territories like Zaporizhzhia and Donbas are more likely targets for Ukrainian gains than heavily fortified Crimea. Track diplomatic signals and Russian domestic pressure as economic exhaustion and massive casualties could create negotiation opportunities. The outcome depends less on who has wonder weapons and more on whether Ukraine can maintain this operational momentum through the summer fighting season.
