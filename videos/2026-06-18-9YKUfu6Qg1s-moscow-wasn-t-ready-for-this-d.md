The attack struck the Moscow oil refinery—one of Russia's ten largest—for the second time in 48 hours, causing massive fires that forced airport closures and highway shutdowns. Russia lacks sufficient air defense systems to protect the entire country, allowing hundreds of drones to penetrate defenses. This attack is part of a sustained Ukrainian strategy exploiting the gap between Russian air defense capabilities and the volume of incoming drones, targeting logistics, refineries, and economic infrastructure. Russian citizens and pro-Russian military bloggers are expressing shock and concern as the war visibly affects Moscow's civilian population.

The attack demonstrates that sustained infrastructure targeting and supply-chain disruption are proving strategically effective, with Russia experiencing fuel shortages for the first time as a major fuel exporter. Monitor Russia's fuel situation and economic pressures, as cascading refinery strikes combined with existing shortages could accelerate logistical collapse and shift Russian domestic opinion on the war.
