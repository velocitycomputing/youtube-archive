## Summary

Laser drone defense systems are effective in specific conditions but face significant real-world challenges. The technology can destroy drones in 10-20 seconds with minimal cost per shot ($1.45-$3) compared to missiles ($50,000+), and Israel recently reported using lasers to intercept 35 Hezbollah drones in combat. However, lasers are limited to line-of-sight engages at roughly 3-mile range, struggle with atmospheric interference (dust, fog, rain), require extended tracking time on target, can only engage one drone at a time, and are vulnerable to hardened or maneuvering drones. A Chinese laser system in Saudi Arabia failed in desert conditions due to dust disruption and excessive cooling requirements.

Treat lasers as one component of layered air defense, not a replacement for other systems. Pair lasers with radar, electronic jamming, guns, interceptor drones, missiles, and decoys. Ships are superior platforms for lasers due to greater power availability, cooling capacity, and space; ground-based systems on trucks are harder to keep operational in harsh conditions and vulnerable to damage. Military procurement should continue laser development, but don't believe defense contractors claiming lasers will make missiles obsolete—effectiveness depends entirely on the specific threat, environment, and what asset you're protecting.
