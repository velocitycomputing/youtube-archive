## Summary

The video presents four exercises that together strengthen all major knee muscles: knee extensions in sitting (targets quads, the brakes of the knee), hamstring bridges (stabilizes the knee), forward lean calf raises (controls knee position), and sit-to-stand progressions (applies strength to daily movements). The instructor emphasizes starting gently and building progressively, using proper mind-muscle connection, and avoiding pain. Each exercise has beginner, intermediate, and advanced variations.

To implement this routine, perform all four exercises three times per week for 10–12 minutes total. Begin with 10–15 repetitions of knee extensions and hamstring bridges (1–2 sets each), 15–20 forward lean calf raises, and 3–10 sit-to-stands (progress from normal to staggered to single-leg using towel stacks under the chair to reduce difficulty). Increase reps and sets only when knees feel good the following day. Stop any exercise that causes pain and gradually remove towels from under the chair as single-leg strength improves.
