## Summary

On May 14th, Russia launched 675 drones and 56 missiles at Kyiv, killing 24 civilians including three children; Ukraine's air defenses intercepted most, but strikes destroyed residential buildings and killed non-combatants. Ukraine responded on May 15th with a large-scale drone strike deep into Russian territory, targeting the Ryazan oil refinery and multiple other military and economic assets simultaneously—hitting facilities 1,000 km inside Russia. The refinery caught fire with explosions continuing for hours, demonstrating Ukraine's new capability: 200+ long-range drones per day, including the Flamingo system carrying 1,100 kg warheads, combined with partnerships with Germany, Britain, Denmark, and Norway to scale production and extend range to 1,500 km. A Russian governor's propaganda claim that Ukrainian drones hit civilian buildings was disproven by his own video, which showed Russian air defenses firing at and destroying a residential building instead.

Russia's oil production has dropped 460,000 barrels per day and fallen to 2009 levels, directly reducing revenue for weapons, soldier salaries, and logistics; Ukraine plans 7 million drones in 2026 as production scales, meaning refinery strikes will intensify and the economic cost to Russia will compound. Understand that Ukrainian targeting claims are verifiable through open-source video evidence while Russian propaganda collapses under scrutiny, and that Ukraine's ability to inflict sustained economic and military damage is expanding, not diminishing.
