## Summary

Dr. Attia discussed how to assess and prevent cognitive decline and neurodegenerative disease. Family history is more revealing than genetic testing for understanding dementia risk; while 25% of the population carries one copy of the APOE4 gene (associated with preventable late-onset Alzheimer's), early-onset forms driven by other genes are less preventable. He identified four evidence-based pillars for maintaining cognition: exercise (with the strongest protective effect), lipid management, avoiding type 2 diabetes, and adequate sleep. Exercise's impact is extraordinary—having top-tier cardiorespiratory fitness and strength reduces mortality risk by 400% and 200% respectively compared to the lowest quartiles, outperforming interventions like smoking cessation and blood pressure control. For heart health specifically, not smoking, maintaining blood pressure at or below 120/80, and keeping APOB (the lipoprotein carrying LDL cholesterol) at physiologic levels are the critical preventive factors.

**Actionable steps:** Know your family history of dementia and get APOE4 testing if indicated. Get a baseline APOB blood test (costs ~$12) and retest three to four times yearly; if results are high, use pharmacologic intervention (anti-lipid therapy) rather than extreme dietary restriction, which creates other problems. Check your blood pressure and keep it at or below 120/80. Prioritize building cardiorespiratory fitness and muscle strength through consistent exercise—this single intervention offers the strongest risk reduction. Maintain adequate sleep and manage stress, as poor sleep and high cortisol damage your heart and brain. The goal is not choosing between interventions but stacking as many protective factors as possible.
