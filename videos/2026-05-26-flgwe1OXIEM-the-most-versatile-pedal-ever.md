## Summary

Graphic equalizers are versatile multi-purpose pedals that can shape your guitar tone, act as clean or treble boosts, enhance overdrives, and transform your overall rig sound. The video demonstrates multiple applications: placing an EQ at the front of your chain to simulate different pickup types (e.g., turning a humbucker into a Gretsch sound), using it as a clean boost by raising the level fader, pairing it with overdrive pedals to exaggerate or cut specific frequencies (particularly useful for scooped-sounding pedals), and placing it after distortion/overdrive sections to surgically EQ each pedal—a technique used by David Gilmour. The presenter also covers different EQ types: graphic sliders (Boss GE-7, MXR 6-band/10-band, X-Acto modified GE-7) and parametric knob-based options (Empress ParaEQ) for more precise frequency control.

If you play covers requiring multiple guitar tones or feel limited by your current overdrive pedals, add a graphic EQ to your board—start with the Boss GE-7 or MXR models, which are budget-friendly and intuitive. Place it before your overdrives to transform your guitar's natural tone, or after overdrives/distortion for tone-shaping and finishing. Pair it with any overdrive to instantly unlock new tonal variations without buying additional pedals. For maximum precision, consider upgrading to a parametric EQ like the Empress ParaEQ if you're comfortable with a steeper learning curve.
