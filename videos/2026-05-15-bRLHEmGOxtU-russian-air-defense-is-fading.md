## Summary

Russian air defense has been systematically degraded, with Ukraine destroying 108 air defense systems, 39 radars, and six electronic warfare systems between December and May. These gaps are enabling Ukrainian drones to conduct daily deep strikes across Russian territory, hitting military targets like aircraft, helicopters, and command posts, as well as economic targets such as the Ryazan oil refinery, which produces 840,000 tons of aviation kerosene annually. Russia has responded with large-scale attacks, including a 1,600-munition barrage that killed 24 civilians in a Kyiv apartment building, and has also attacked UN humanitarian vehicles in Kherson while bragging about the strikes on Telegram, claiming they were legitimate dual-use military targets.

If you're tracking the conflict or supporting Ukraine, the actionable takeaway is that air defense attrition is creating cascading vulnerabilities for Russia that will persist—each destroyed system opens gaps that Ukraine exploits, and even intercepted drones provide targeting data for future waves.
