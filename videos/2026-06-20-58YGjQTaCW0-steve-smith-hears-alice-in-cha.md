## Summary

Steve Smith, the legendary Journey drummer and Vital Information founder, participated in Drumo's "Hears For The First Time" series, where he was given a drumless version of "Wood" by Alice in Chains without knowing the song's identity. He analyzed the track, noting its groovy bass line, tempo variations between verses and choruses, and dynamic shifts, then performed three takes that progressively became more expressive and adventurous—incorporating tom work, triplets, and double bass patterns inspired by Mitch Mitchell. After revealing the song's origin and playing Sean Kenny's original drum part, Steve praised Kenny's syncopation and tom work, ultimately concluding that while creating an alternative interpretation was fun and valid, the original drum tracks possess an inherent musicality that comes from the band's collective creative process in the room.

Viewers interested in Steve Smith's drumming can access exclusive free courses on jazz drumming and drum soloing through the Drumo platform (linked in the video description), and can watch a related video on Muzora's YouTube channel featuring Steve and Vital Information performing a spontaneous cover of Sound Garden's "Black Hole Sun"—a full-band version of the same type of creative exercise shown here.
