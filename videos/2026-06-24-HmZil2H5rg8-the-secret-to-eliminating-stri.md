## Summary

The video features instructor Michael addressing Brian's question about eliminating unwanted string noise while performing bends from a Gregg Allman song. The core issue is that extra strings ring out unintentionally during full bends and fast legato runs, creating unwanted noise. Michael's solution: proper muting is the key, achieved by practicing with high volume and high gain/distortion to make the guitar extremely sensitive to every unwanted sound. When mistakes become audibly obvious, your hands naturally train themselves to mute all strings you're not playing through coordinated effort from both the fretting hand and picking hand. Michael also emphasizes using stronger fingers (index, middle, ring) for big bends rather than the weaker pinky, and notes that establishing a resting position that automatically dampens unused strings is essential.

To eliminate string noise, practice with volume and gain turned up high so you can hear every mistake clearly, forcing your muscle memory to develop proper muting instinctively. Work in 10-minute sessions, 2-3 times daily for a couple weeks, prioritizing muting technique over playing speed. Make muting a constant commitment by consciously dampening every string you're not playing using both hands, and establish a resting picking-hand position that naturally mutes unused strings. Use your stronger fingers for bends instead of your pinky. The method works because connecting auditory feedback (hearing the mistakes) to your physical feel trains your hands to mute correctly through repetition and sound-based learning.
