## Summary

Emad Mostaque argues that AI will make human cognitive labor economically irrelevant within 3 years. He outlines that AI already outperforms humans at medical diagnosis, coding competitions, and mathematical reasoning, with pricing dropping to $0.50-$1 per million tokens (compared to $100,000+ annual salaries). By 2026, AI agents will replace screen-based jobs; by 2027-2028, digital copies of workers trained on company emails and Slack messages will replace the originals. He presents three futures: regional AI fragmentation, corporate mega-control (where GPU ownership determines power), or symbiotic human-AI augmentation. The core issue is that companies with fiduciary responsibility will choose AI at 1% of labor cost, making traditional employment economics obsolete for cognitive work.

To prepare, start using AI daily (ChatGPT, Claude) to develop competency and stay ahead of disruption. If you manage a team, mandate one hour per day of AI tool usage to improve company output. Stop defining yourself solely by your job title; instead, build resilience through networks, family, community, and meaningful relationships—these will become your actual source of value. If starting a company, focus on distribution and leverage rather than hiring; AI lets you replicate software and services in weeks, enabling thin teams to compete with enterprises. Ultimately, treat 2026 as a deadline to reframe what work and meaning look like in your life.
