## Summary

Anthony Mangieri, owner of Una Pizza Napoletana (twice voted world's best pizza), documents a full service day in NYC where he personally makes every pizza and dessert. He begins at 8–9 a.m. making fresh sourdough dough using naturally fermented starter, 4–8 types of Italian flour, high-hydration water, and Sicilian sea salt—adjusting recipes daily based on temperature and environment. He fires up a 30-year-old handmade Italian wood-burning oven with birch and oak for three hours, sources buffalo mozzarella drained of excess liquid, and makes fresh fruit sorbetto and cremolada (Italian ice cream) daily without stabilizers. During service with one assistant, he and Sean operate the pizza station at maximum efficiency, building and baking 130–150 pizzas over five hours, with each pizza taking roughly one minute to top and two minutes to bake, always selling out.

If you visit Una, expect a line since it opens only three days per week and never has inventory left. If making pizza at home, use naturally fermented sourdough starter instead of commercial yeast, prioritize high-hydration dough (lots of water creates air), handle the dough gently without deflating it, source premium ingredients (especially tomatoes and mozzarella), and invest in proper tools like a quality peel. For sorbet, make fresh daily without stabilizers, add acid like lemon to prevent a baby-food texture, and use a proper spinning machine. Mangieri emphasizes that excellence comes from daily refinement and willingness to work 15+ physically demanding hours repeatedly.
