## Summary

Oliver Tree discussed his self-produced album "Love You Madly Hate You Badly," which he created by recording in 82 countries over two years while traveling solo through hotel rooms. He detailed how this approach freed him from exploitative label contracts that left him making only 14 cents per dollar, while allowing him to capture genuine memories and experiences instead of studio sessions. He explained the album's themes of love and hate through a turbulent relationship, referenced specific songs like "Joyride" (drum and bass influenced by Eastern European brutalist architecture), "Halo" (embracing sexuality as part of the love/loss narrative), and "Glow On" (inspired by watching someone find their life purpose). He also shared that he's creating a 200-page photography book from the journey, a documentary filmed in Antarctica, and now producing a deluxe version with 17 complete remixes—noting the self-production process requires multiple iterations (50-60 versions of some songs) and is exhaustingly difficult.

For creators feeling burnt out: consider stepping back from commercial pressure to pursue related passions that might reignite your original drive (Oliver rediscovered his love for music through photography). If self-producing your art, budget substantial time for iteration and be prepared for the work to take far longer than expected. Prioritize collaborating with people you enjoy over prestigious names, and remember that capturing real emotions and genuine experiences often produces better work than pursuing commercial polish.
