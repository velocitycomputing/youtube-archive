## Summary

**What Was Discussed:**
The 1979 BBC program explores word processors as transformative technology for offices and home working. Word processors are computers dedicated to text handling that eliminate manual retyping, filing, and postal systems—the transistor enables instant editing, moving paragraphs, correcting mistakes, and storing files on magnetic disks. Real examples demonstrate impact: Bradford Metropolitan Council's Wordplex system halved staff needs while increasing departmental output 40%; a mortgage transaction requiring a typist's 2.5 hours now takes one person 2 minutes. Texas Instruments' portable bubble memory terminal connects to head office via telephone, enabling remote work. F International's Linda English demonstrates practical remote programming from home. France's visionary approach: providing 30 million free home terminals to citizens. The program predicts a worldwide networked information society where word processors communicate with each other, eliminating intermediaries, and shows alternative input devices—Micropad (handwriting recognition) and the one-handed Microwriter—making typing accessible. The future vision: industrial revolution turning full circle from centralized factories back to distributed cottage industries enabled by silicon chip technology.

**What's Actionable:**
Organizations should implement word processors immediately to realize 40-60% cost savings and staff reductions while increasing output. Knowledge workers should prepare for remote work: the technology to work from home already exists and eliminates commuting. Employers should develop distributed work policies now—companies like F International prove multiple workers can operate cost-effectively from home using just a telephone line and terminal. Individuals should learn the new input methods (typing, Microwriter) as keyboard skills will become essential for future employment. Policymakers should recognize that eliminating the "middle person" in networked communication will fundamentally change organizational structure and employment—advance planning for this shift is critical to avoid negative consequences while maximizing benefits to individual freedom and decentralization.
