# Summary

Over the course of one year, a couple with no renovation experience purchased a severely derelict 500-year-old thatched cottage in the Cotswolds and documented their journey restoring it while working full-time jobs. The property had catastrophic water damage, mold, rot, mushrooms, dead rodents, failing electrics, and a completely compromised thatched roof. After initial cleanup and installing a heat pump (costing £10,500 with a government grant), they faced their biggest decision: rethatch the roof for £77,000 or tile it locally with handmade tiles for significantly less. They chose tiling, stripped the old thatch with help from family and friends, installed insulation, addressed structural concerns from engineers, and hired professional roofers (Mike and Reuben) to complete the work. By year's end, the roof was finished, they'd moved in, discovered a centuries-old well, and learned they were expecting a baby—turning what was meant to be their defining accomplishment into one part of a much larger life transformation.

For anyone considering a major renovation: secure government grants upfront, prioritize the roof before other work, budget significantly more than initial estimates, verify contractor work with independent professionals (not just the contractor's sign-off), plan for projects to take 2-3 times longer than expected, recruit family and friends for labor-intensive demolition phases, and build in contingency funds—they'll be needed when hidden structural problems emerge.
