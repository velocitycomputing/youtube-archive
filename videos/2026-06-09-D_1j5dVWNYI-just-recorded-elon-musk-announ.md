## Summary

SpaceX announced an ambitious plan to help humanity advance on the Kardashev scale—a measure of civilizational power use—by launching AI data center satellites into orbit. The discussion identified three limiting factors: mass-to-orbit capacity (addressed by Starship, which will increase launches from 2,500 tons/year to millions within 3 years), solar power generation, and AI chips. The first AI satellite design produces 150kW peak/120kW sustained power using solar panels and radiators based on existing Starlink technology. SpaceX plans to scale to 1 gigawatt/year of space-based AI compute by end of 2027, 10 gigawatts by mid-2028, and eventually a terawatt/year via a massive new "terafab" chip factory (100 million sq ft—10× Tesla's Gigafactory Texas). Beyond Earth, they envision manufacturing on the moon and using an electromagnetic mass driver to launch satellites into deep space without rockets.

For interested parties, SpaceX is actively hiring for AI satellite production at their Bastrop, Texas facility, which is expanding now with operational solar manufacturing and satellite construction already underway. The technology relies on proven components from existing Starlink satellites, making it achievable with current engineering. The company targets delivering operational AI satellites to orbit in the near term, with the moon-based mass driver as a longer-term infrastructure goal to achieve orders-of-magnitude greater power scaling.
