## Summary

Ukraine is employing three categories of drone warfare: a "drone wall" on the front line, mid-range tactical strikes targeting Russian logistics and command centers 20-150km away, and long-range strategic strikes deep into Russia. The mid-range strikes have been particularly effective at creating a logistics lockdown, especially in southern Ukraine. By targeting the Kerch Strait bridge, ferries, maritime ports (Mariupol and Berdyansk), and the M14 highway, Ukraine has severely disrupted supply routes to Crimea and Russian-occupied territories, causing fuel shortages and forcing Russian units to rely on fragile supply lines.

The Kherson region and western Zaporizhzhia region represent Russia's most vulnerable occupied areas due to these supply constraints. If Ukraine's mid-range drone campaign continues degrading Russian logistics, the southern front could become an active flashpoint where Ukraine attempts to cut the land corridor—potentially isolating Russian forces and creating conditions for territorial gains, similar to its failed 2023 counter-offensive. Monitor this region closely, as the Zaporizhzhia nuclear power plant could become strategically significant if supply line disruptions force Russian defensive choices.
