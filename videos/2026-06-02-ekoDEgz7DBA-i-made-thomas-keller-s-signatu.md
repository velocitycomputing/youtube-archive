## Summary

Jamie launches a new series called "Jamie and Chef" where he cooks six signature dishes from a different chef's cookbook each season, starting with Thomas Keller's "The French Laundry." His first dish is Oysters and Pearls—a fine-dining signature that combines shucked oysters atop a sabayon-enriched pearl tapioca custard, garnished with Osetra caviar and a butter sauce made with vermouth, white wine vinegar, and shallots. The episode walks through every step: shucking 16 oysters (substituting blue points for unavailable Malpecs), soaking and cooking small pearl tapioca until it thickens to a pudding-like consistency, whisking egg yolks and oyster juice over simmering water to create the sabayon, whipping heavy cream, and building a rich butter sauce. He invests in 1 ounce of expensive Osetra caviar ($80) and finishes by heating the assembled dish in the oven, topping it with two oysters, sauce, minced chives, and a quenelle of caviar.

The dish is reproducible at home if you commit to sourcing quality ingredients and mastering the techniques. You'll need fresh oysters, small pearl tapioca (soaked an hour before), heavy cream, eggs, butter, dry vermouth, white wine vinegar, shallots, fresh chives, and Osetra caviar—the latter being the major cost at roughly $80 for 1 ounce. The core techniques are straightforward but timing-sensitive: shuck oysters carefully to preserve juice, cook tapioca until it leaves a trail and becomes translucent, whisk the sabayon over heat until it holds a ribbon, fold in whipped cream and season conservatively, then execute the final assembly with oysters, sauce, and caviar topping just before serving.
