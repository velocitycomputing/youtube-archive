## Summary

Navy SEAL Kenny Bigbee Jr. taught Jesse combat principles rather than specific techniques, emphasizing that effective fighting relies on emergent patterns and combat concepts. The core lessons included using bisecting lines (crossing defensive patterns) to defend, moving to the opponent's outside line (positioning behind them) to gain control, and understanding that all fighting systems share common fundamentals like footwork, fulcrums, and leverage. Kenny outlined three training layers: technical (perfect form), tactical (finding opportunities in chaos), and scenario-specific (fighting while exhausted). Jesse then completed a field training exercise combining obstacle courses with armed combatives, where he successfully applied these concepts under extreme fatigue, learning that real-world performance won't be clean but adapting principles creates "clarity out of chaos."

For practical application, prioritize offense over defense to actually win fights rather than just survive them. In armed scenarios, use bisecting line defensive patterns combined with two-on-one grips to control an opponent's weapon arm and move to the outside line (their back). Adopt the "until" mindset—keep fighting until you're too tired to continue, free yourself, or achieve victory. Train these principles under fatigue and chaos rather than in controlled settings, since real stress prevents you from remembering specific techniques but makes simple concepts like "defend with a bisecting line" repeatable. Always assume weapons are present and practice ground-fighting defenses accordingly, avoiding positions like the mount that leave you vulnerable to a blade.
