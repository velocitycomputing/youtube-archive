## Summary

The reviewer tested the newly refreshed Yamaha RevStar lineup (available in Professional, Standard ~$799, and Element ~$400-500 tiers) and examined two Standard models—one with humbuckers and one with P90s. Key specs include stainless steel frets, carbon-reinforced neck, satin finish on the back, mahogany body with maple top, and a chambered design for optimal balance (7.5–8 lbs). The electronics feature a unique five-way switch with coil-delay technology in positions 2 and 4, plus a tone control that re-voices the pickup rather than doing a traditional coil split. Sound testing revealed the guitars deliver excellent clean tones with a powerful, dynamic tone control, great sustain with the humbucker neck pickup, and gritty, versatile P90 tones with gain applied. Compared to the Pacifica, the RevStar represents a major upgrade in fretwork quality (stainless steel instead of standard frets) and switch durability, though the pots remain small.

If you want a guitar with P90s without spending heavily, the P90 RevStar is an excellent choice; the Standard model's ~$799 price includes premium features (stainless steel frets, stable carbon-reinforced neck) usually reserved for much more expensive guitars. The three-tier lineup means you can start at entry-level (~$400-500) or invest in the mid-range Standard for professional-grade playability and tone. This guitar is worth buying if you're seeking a well-made alternative to Fender or Gibson that offers sonic versatility and won't require constant neck adjustments.
