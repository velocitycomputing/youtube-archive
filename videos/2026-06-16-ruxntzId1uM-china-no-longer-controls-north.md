## Summary

**What Was Discussed**
Peter Zeihan traced North Korea's evolution from a Soviet-dependent satellite state under Kim Il-sung and the chaotic rule of Kim Jong-il, to Kim Jong-un's strategic consolidation of power by eliminating competing generals. Zeihan explained how Kim Jong-un deliberately sought independence from Chinese control after humiliating summits with Xi Jinping, initially by engaging Trump (Singapore and Vietnam summits in 2017-2019), but finding his real leverage through Russia when Russia was isolated by the Ukraine war in 2022. North Korea deployed over 10,000 troops to fight in Ukraine and received advanced weaponry including intercontinental ballistic missiles in return. Most significantly, Kim Jong-un recently rewrote North Korea's constitution to formally declare itself an independent state no longer seeking Korean reunification—a historic shift that transforms North Korea into an autonomous geopolitical player with nuclear capability, advanced ballistic missiles, and no master.

**What's Actionable**
The immediate takeaway is that North Korea is no longer a controlled proxy of any major power but an unpredictable wild card now capable of striking the continental United States, with all of South Korea and Japan in its strike zone. For policy makers and investors in East Asia, this means the old assumptions about Beijing controlling Pyongyang are obsolete; the strategic calculus for Japan, South Korea, and the U.S. must account for an independent, armed, and demonstrably opportunistic North Korea that has proven willing to extract value from great-power conflicts. The current Xi-Kim summit reflects Beijing's failure to retain influence, signaling that regional instability will likely increase as North Korea plays rivals against each other while consolidating weapons capabilities.
