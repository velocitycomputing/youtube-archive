## Summary

Ukrainian forces have conducted a sustained campaign of drone strikes on Russian-occupied Crimea, blacking out the major city of Sevastopol by targeting the Balaklava thermal power plant and attacking fuel tanks, other power facilities, and port infrastructure across the peninsula. These strikes are part of a deliberate logistics strategy to cut off Crimea from the Russian mainland and render it unusable as a military hub by degrading its electricity, port capacity, and supply lines; Russia has responded with a failed attempt to build a dirt causeway as a desperation measure. The campaign extends beyond Crimea to target Moscow's largest oil refinery, crippling 40-50% of Moscow's fuel supplies and compounding economic pressure through rising inflation and falling wages—a squeeze designed to pressure both Russian civilians and the oligarchs who prop up Putin's government.

The strikes have gained unexpected diplomatic support from Donald Trump, who privately called Ukraine's long-range strike campaign "hugely impressive" at the G7 and agreed to increase sanctions on Russian energy; US intelligence is reportedly supporting these operations, though the US has stopped short of providing Patriot missile batteries. For those wanting to support Ukraine directly, the video references an active fundraiser partnering with Ukraine Aid Operations (a US 501c3) that provides long-range strike drones, ground logistics drones, and heavy-lift transport to active units—with donor tiers starting at $15-30.
