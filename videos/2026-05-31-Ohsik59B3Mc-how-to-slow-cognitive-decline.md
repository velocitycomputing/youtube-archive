## Summary

Drs. Attia and Huberman discussed Alzheimer's disease as the most prevalent neurodegenerative condition affecting 6 million Americans. While age is the primary risk factor, they examined genetic components including the ApoE gene variants and rare deterministic genes (PSEN1, PSEN2, APP) that guarantee disease development by age 50. The conversation highlighted how the amyloid-beta hypothesis, long central to Alzheimer's research, has come under scrutiny—a deliberately falsified landmark paper derailed the field for a decade, and amyloid-targeting drugs have largely failed despite amyloid plaques appearing in healthy autopsy cases. Unlike cardiovascular medicine, which has biomarkers and proven interventions, Alzheimer's disease lacks a precision medicine approach.

To protect cognitive health, the evidence points to four unequivocal factors you can control: prioritize adequate sleep, maintain low LDL cholesterol and ApoB levels, preserve insulin sensitivity (avoid type II diabetes), and exercise regularly. If time-constrained to three hours per week, allocate one hour to low-intensity cardio, one hour to strength training, and one hour to interval training for maximum cognitive protection.
