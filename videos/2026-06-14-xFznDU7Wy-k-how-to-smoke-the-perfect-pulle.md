## Summary

Aaron Franklin demonstrates how to smoke a pork butt for approximately 10 hours total: 8 hours unwrapped at 270°F to develop a thick, crusty bark, then wrapped tightly in foil for the final 1.5–2 hours. He uses a simple, clean rub of equal parts kosher salt and black pepper with paprika for color, applied generously over a mustard binder. Starting around the 3-hour mark, he spritzes the meat hourly with unsweetened liquid (apple cider vinegar, water, or beer) to keep edges moist. The critical visual cue for wrapping is when the fat cap splits, which typically occurs around 8 hours. Doneness is judged primarily by feel—the meat should feel tender and slightly squishy when probed—rather than strict temperature targets, though 203–206°F is ideal. The meat rests for 45 minutes to an hour before pulling, at which point the bone should slide out easily.

To execute this at home, start with a bone-in pork butt, apply mustard generously, then coat heavily with your salt-and-pepper rub (more seasoning is forgiving and mixes into the final product). Maintain your cooker at 270°F with a water pan, place the meat fat-side up, and spritz every hour from hour 3 onward. Watch for the fat cap to split as your wrapping signal. Once wrapped, cook another 1.5–2 hours, then test tenderness by inserting a probe—it should meet slight resistance rather than push through easily. The forgiving nature of this cut means slight variations still yield excellent results.
