## Summary

The instructor demonstrates how to enhance basic triad shapes by adding the second (or ninth) note to create melodic improvisation without relying on scales. He shows that the second is found two frets above the root note and can be incorporated into root position, first inversion, and second inversion triads across the three string sets. Additional techniques discussed include playing double stops between the second and third, using string bends from root to second, and finding natural musical phrases by moving triad shapes through chord progressions, drawing inspiration from musicians like John Coltrane who use chord tones and light embellishments rather than complex scales.

To apply this approach, practice locating the second in each of your existing triad shapes and experiment playing them through a chord progression like the D-C-Em-D example shown. Work through the three triad inversions with the added second, then explore combining the shapes with double stops (especially the major second interval between the second and third) and bends. Finally, take these techniques to the remaining string sets that weren't covered in the video, trying each position to discover which licks and musical possibilities feel natural to your playing style.
