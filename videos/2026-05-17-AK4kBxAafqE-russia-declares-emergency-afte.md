## Summary

Over 500 Ukrainian drones attacked Russia in a coordinated assault spanning 10 PM to 7 AM, forcing Russia to declare a national emergency and hitting targets across an area larger than Texas. Strikes targeted Moscow's Sheremetyevo Airport (canceling hundreds of flights), the Moscow oil refinery, fuel storage at Dvorichna, military-industrial nodes in Bryansk, multiple Crimean airbases including Belbek, and a microelectronics facility in Zelenograd. Russia claimed to shoot down 556 drones, but many penetrated defenses—Moscow alone reported 73–120 downed in 24 hours. The attack was the largest of the war, demonstrating Ukraine's ability to overwhelm Russian air defense through drone saturation tactics, with intelligence suggesting Russian opposition groups like Tash assisted using electronic warfare.

The attacks reveal three immediate consequences: First, Ukraine has mapped Russian air defense weaknesses and will continue targeting them with larger follow-up swarms, making Russia unable to stop major incursions. Second, Russia must now spread its military thin defending Russian territory, degrading its offensive capacity in Ukraine and making it harder to hold Crimea—a loss Putin cannot survive politically. Third, Putin's regime is in acute crisis; he faces pressure from frustrated elites, mounting billions in war costs, failed military objectives, and demonstrated inability to protect Russia itself. Expect more attacks and potential territorial losses as Ukraine exploits Russia's exhausted defenses and crumbling state capacity.
