## Summary

The video teaches a two-phase ace trick performable with any borrowed, shuffled deck. A spectator selects a card (e.g., Queen of Hearts) and returns it to the deck. The performer secretly notes the bottom card (key card), controls the spectator's card to the top using an overhand shuffle and optional Charlier shuffle, then removes the four aces while using a finger break technique to keep the selected card on top. The trick progresses through an "elevator sequence" where aces appear to travel up and down the deck, followed by a second phase where the performer forces the spectator to point to a face-down card, which turns out to be their original selection while the aces vanish.

Practice the Charlier shuffle for deceptive mixing, learn the elevator sequence count (which counts five cards as four), and memorize the sequence of dealing aces face-up and face-down.
