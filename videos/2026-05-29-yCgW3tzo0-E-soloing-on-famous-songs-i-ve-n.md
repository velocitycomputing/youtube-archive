## Summary

Alex improvises guitar solos on four songs he's never heard: Periphery, Red Hot Chili Peppers' "Dani California," Slipknot, and Animals as Leaders. After each blind improvisation, Fabio plays the original solo and they analyze the differences. The conversation covers Alex's discovery of fuzz effects and how tone dramatically changes a song's feel, plus an extended music theory lesson on modal scales—specifically how using Lydian, Dorian, and other modes changes only one or two notes but creates completely different emotional colors and harmonic flavors.

To apply this: if you play guitar, try the same challenge with unfamiliar songs to train your ear and improve improvisation. Experiment with modal scales by keeping the same root note and changing one note at a time (E minor to E Lydian, for example) to hear how profoundly it shifts the sound. If you don't have deep familiarity with a song or style, plan 30 minutes to an hour of focused listening before attempting to play or compose in that style. Finally, if you haven't experimented with fuzz or other effects, test them on your playing—they fundamentally transform how your solos sit in a mix.
