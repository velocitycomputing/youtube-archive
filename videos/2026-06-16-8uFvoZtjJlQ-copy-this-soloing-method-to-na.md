## Summary

The video teaches a four-step "solo mapping method" to overcome common soloing problems like mindlessly copying tabs or noodling. Step 1: Understand the harmony by identifying the chord progression and key (demonstrated with "Stand By Me": A–F#m–D–E, or 1–6–4–5 in A major). Step 2: Select connected scale positions on the fretboard as your working area. Step 3: Map the chord triads within those scale positions as landmarks. Step 4: Solo using the scale while landing phrases on chord tones from the triads. The key insight is that "resolving" phrases to chord tones—not just wandering aimlessly—is what makes soloing sound musical and helps you follow the chord progression.

Practice the method immediately by looping "Stand By Me" (or using the backing track linked in the video description) and working through all four steps. Start by playing just the triad shapes as a rhythm guitarist to internalize the chord changes, then progress to playing one key note from each triad, and finally add scale notes around those anchor points. For deeper instruction, the instructor's school community offers a step-by-step roadmap covering how to identify keys from progressions, map triads across the fretboard, and turn this into melodic solos.
