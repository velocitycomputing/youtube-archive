## Summary

Justin, a Nashville session guitarist, teaches how to play guitar fills without stepping on the vocal—a skill mastered by all great guitar players. He demonstrates using a drum loop, simple chord progression, and a "vocal" melody, showing that the key is phrasing your fills opposite to the main melody. Like a conversation, if you talk over someone instead of letting them finish before responding, it sounds cluttered. He reveals specific techniques: identify gaps in the vocal waveform where notes sustain, offset your fills to hit around beats 2-3 when the vocal hits beat 1, and avoid landing on the same note the vocalist will sing.

To practice, start by playing fills around someone else's singing (easier than doing both simultaneously), then build toward singing and playing together. Use simple arpeggiation of chord tones between vocal phrases rather than complex licks—these work better when you're also singing. Visually examining vocal waveforms shows exactly where the gaps are, making it easier to find your moments. The goal is weaving in and out conversationally: let the vocal phrase finish, scoot in while its note is still hanging, and pull back before the next phrase starts.
