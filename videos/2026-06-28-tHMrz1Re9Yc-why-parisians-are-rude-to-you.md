## Summary

The video explains how to navigate Paris restaurants successfully, starting with identifying tourist traps—which feature big red awnings near major attractions, multi-cuisine menus, English signage, and staff soliciting customers outside—versus legitimate restaurants that have limited menus, French-speaking diners, and handwritten chalkboard menus showing what the chef found fresh that day. It then covers essential behavioral rules: always greet staff with "bonjour" first, ask permission before sitting, never snap fingers or demand service, and avoid asking for substitutions (chefs take pride in their dishes). The video addresses common financial mistakes: tap water is free if you ask for "carafe d'eau"; meal prices typically range €12-28 depending on the restaurant and seating location; tips are already included by law (just round up the bill); and there are no refills on any drinks. It also explains that restaurants operate on split schedules (lunch 12-2:30pm, dinner 7-10pm), formula menus offer fixed prices for multiple courses on weekdays, and bottled water costs €5-6 per bottle.

To avoid being marked as a difficult tourist, arrive at noon or 7pm to guarantee seating and faster service, order from the formula menu during lunch (confirming selections are included), request carafe d'eau or specify flat/sparkling bottled water clearly to avoid surprise charges, and accept dishes as presented without modifications unless you have allergies (which you can show in Google Translate). If unsure which restaurant to try, use the creator's guide of 125 vetted Paris restaurants. Fundamentally, respect the chef's expertise and the server's time, speak basic French politeness terms, and remember that paying €60 for steak-frites is indeed being overcharged—quality meals cost €15-30.
