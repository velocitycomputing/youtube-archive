## Summary

Fender won a German court ruling granting exclusive rights to the Strat body shape in the EU and sent cease-and-desist letters to competing guitar makers. A leaked video of CEO Bud Cole's private dealer presentation became a viral PR disaster, featuring poorly delivered corporate jargon defending the campaign with little conviction. The creator criticizes Cole's lack of guitar knowledge and poor communication skills as CEO. Most significantly, Tomman—the world's largest music retailer—is now legally challenging Fender's position, escalating this from a small-builder dispute to a major corporate showdown that could reshape the industry.

To take action, withdraw support from Fender until they reverse course and ideally replace CEO Bud Cole, whose departure would signal the company recognizes its mistake. Support alternative brands like PRS, Ibanez, Charvel, and others that continue making Strat-style guitars, demonstrating market preference for competition over monopoly. Monitor the Tomman legal challenge closely, as its outcome will likely determine whether Fender's shape monopoly holds in court and may invalidate the entire enforcement campaign. The unified opposition from guitar players and major retailers represents the strongest counter-pressure to stop Fender's overreach.
