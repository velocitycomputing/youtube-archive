## Summary

Ukraine is systematically isolating Crimea by targeting overland supply routes with long-range drones, forcing Russian logistics to take longer alternate routes that bring convoys closer to Ukrainian strike range. Russia historically relied on two northern overland routes for 95% of Crimea's supplies, but Ukrainian attacks on bridges like Chongar are compelling Russia to stretch its supply lines westward by 100+ kilometers. The Kerch Bridge remains the primary eastern route but Russian sources indicate it will likely become the next priority target. Alternative supply routes—sea (currently 4% of supplies) and air (1%)—are severely limited: the Black Sea and Sea of Azov have no new Russian ships available and ports are under attack, while Russia's ~120 IL-76 transport aircraft lack the capacity to fly 10,000 monthly sorties needed to fully sustain Crimea. Russia is deploying air defense assets to intercept drones but faces a critical shortage: these systems must be reallocated from protecting other strategic targets, creating a zero-sum dilemma across occupied territory.

The immediate takeaway is that Russia faces a logistics collapse in Crimea if it loses the northern routes—the Kerch Bridge becomes the only viable overland alternative, but defending it requires withdrawing air defense from refineries and weapons plants elsewhere. For those monitoring this conflict, watch whether Russia concentrates air defense around critical bridges (a viable hardpoint strategy but leaving other targets exposed) or whether Ukraine can sustain drone strike pressure faster than Russia can deploy countermeasures. The 104th Brigade fundraiser in the video demonstrates how donated vehicles are actively supporting Ukrainian operations at the front.
