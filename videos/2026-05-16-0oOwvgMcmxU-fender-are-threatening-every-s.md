## Summary

Fender launched aggressive legal action against multiple legitimate guitar manufacturers across Europe, sending cease-and-desist letters demanding €250,000 fines and destruction of inventory for companies making Stratocaster-style body guitars. The action targets established brands including Sire, Schecter, Yamaha Pacifica, PRS (Silver Sky), LSL Guitars, Ibanez, ESP, Maybach, FGN, and Harley Benton—not just counterfeiters. Companies must respond by late May, and the threat extends beyond headstock design to the entire Stratocaster body shape, potentially setting a precedent for other iconic designs like Telecasters.

If you're interested in non-Fender Strat-style guitars, consider purchasing through Thomann or directly from manufacturers soon, as these products may be removed from sale. Larger brands like Yamaha and Ibanez may pursue class action legal challenges, but smaller boutique makers lack resources to fight. Monitor whether major retailers take action, as Thomann's involvement in EU sales gives them leverage. Long-term, expect limited affordable alternatives to Fender if the lawsuit succeeds—the speaker warns this threatens consumer choice and innovation that currently drives the market, particularly in features Fender doesn't offer (stainless steel frets, specific neck profiles, Floyd Rose options).
