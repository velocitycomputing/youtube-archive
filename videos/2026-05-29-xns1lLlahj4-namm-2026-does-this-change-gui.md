## Summary

Shark Guitars, a Turkish company with seven years of R&D, showcased revolutionary guitar innovations at NAMM 2026. Their signature "convertible bridge" tremolo system keeps all strings in tune when bending individual strings and switches to fixed mode with full-range tuners for precise pitch adjustment. The guitar features a quick-release neck system (removable in seconds for travel), modular pickup architecture allowing any brand pickups without soldering, built-in three-pedal effects system (distortion, boost, filter) with per-string controls, and a game-changing patent-pending fret system where each fret can be individually calibrated for height and action under string tension—eliminating traditional fret work entirely. All components are titanium for precision, lightweight durability, and color permanence.

To get one of these guitars, visit SharkGuitars.com to design your custom guitar using their 3D designer tool, or try to purchase an off-the-shelf model. If you're a guitar manufacturer or boutique luthier, they're actively seeking OEM partners through their "ocean environment" community program to license this technology. The convertible bridge is expected to become industry standard, particularly in the metal world, so watch for this design appearing on other brands' guitars soon.
