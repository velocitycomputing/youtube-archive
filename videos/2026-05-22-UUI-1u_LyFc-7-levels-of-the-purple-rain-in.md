# Summary: 7 LEVELS of the PURPLE RAIN Intro

Mike from the Art of Guitar demonstrates seven progressive versions of Prince's "Purple Rain" intro to mark the song's 39th anniversary. Level 1 uses basic open chords (G, Em, D, C) with a capo; Level 2 adds power chords; Level 3 expands to full bar chords. Levels 4–5 introduce sus chords with thumb techniques and transitional notes between changes. Level 6 teaches the authentic version without a capo (Bbsus2 over D and related chords), drawing from how Wendy from the Revolution plays it. Level 7 adds advanced arpeggio techniques, playing individual notes before full chords and using muting and note separation for the original recording's character.

Players should start at the level matching their current ability—beginners begin with Level 1's simple capo-based progression, intermediate players practice Levels 4–5 to build thumb dexterity and transitions, and advanced guitarists learn Levels 6–7 to nail the authentic Prince arrangement. Each level builds on previous skills, so progression is linear; the instructor plans a follow-up video on the solo and hints at bringing in a former member of Prince's band.
