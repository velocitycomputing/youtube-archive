## Summary

The discussion centered on the "organizational singularity"—a fundamental restructuring of companies from hierarchy-based human workflows to AI-native agentic workflows. The speakers argued that Ronald Coase's 1937 economic theory justifying large companies has been broken by AI: when two people with open-source tools can replicate a high-margin business line in 60-90 days, traditional coordination models collapse. They presented a new architecture with six layers (purpose, sensing, interpretation, decision, orchestration, learning) wrapped in governance protocols, modeled after military OODA loops, where agents operate with constraints ("passports"), searchable logs, granular rollback, and human oversight to prevent going rogue. Protective competitive modes still exist: proprietary data, regulatory capture, learning velocity advantage, deep customer relationships, and brand loyalty.

For leaders, the core actionable: immediately audit whether small teams using AI could disrupt any of your high-margin lines. If yes, restructuring is urgent—this transformation happens in 1-2 years, not 5-10. Specifically, rebuild your organization around AI workflows where C-suite provides validation (not decisions), middle management shifts 90% from coordination to problem-solving and efficiency, and agents handle routine execution. Expect 75-80% workforce reduction per existing firm, but offset by a Cambrian explosion of 5x more startups. Build your competitive moat in learning velocity, proprietary data, or irreplaceable customer relationships. Organizations not restructuring face disruption; those restructuring early compound advantages as agents recursively self-improve workflows.
