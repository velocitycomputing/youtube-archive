## Summary

The real AI shift is not about model intelligence getting smarter, but about context access. OpenAI's restricted ChatGPT 5.6 release, Apple's redesigned Siri with access to your calendar/email/photos/apps, Anthropic's Claude Tag in Slack (giving Claude access to selected channels and codebases), and OpenAI's Codeex study (showing Codeex became the dominant work tool once it earned trust) all point to the same battle: who controls access to your personal and professional context. With government slowdowns on frontier models, companies are racing to extract more utility from existing AI by connecting it seamlessly to where your actual work and information lives, rather than forcing you to manually copy-paste everything into chat windows.

You should audit what context you're comfortable sharing with each AI company (Apple's personal data, Anthropic's work data, OpenAI's code/files) and decide if you want to retain control rather than lock into one provider. Stop waiting for the next model release—instead, maximize the value of current models by setting up integration points: tag Claude into your Slack channels, give Siri access to your phone's context, point Codeex at your local work files. If you don't want to be trapped with a single company, invest time building a personal harness that lets you route different contexts to different AI tools based on your comfort level and use case.
