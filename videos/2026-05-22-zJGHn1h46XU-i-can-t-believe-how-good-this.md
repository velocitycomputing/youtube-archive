## Summary

Luke Cyrus, a lap steel guitarist who gained fame on Instagram, discussed his Duesenberg lap steel and how to play it. He explained his custom tuning—a hybrid combining a G chord (GBD) with open D—which yields three chords without pedals and is rich with harmonic intervals. He demonstrated the multi-bender pedal system that raises or lowers strings by half steps, explained his use of a volume pedal for expression, and described his Lollar pickups (a 1940s design predating modern humbuckers). He also shared that NYXL strings outlast regular strings significantly, discussed the importance of precise tuning calibration (recounting how he was tuning to 441Hz instead of 440Hz), and noted that the electric lap steel historically preceded the electric guitar.

To start with lap steel, you can add a nut extender to a regular guitar to convert it into one, or purchase multi-benders to add to an existing instrument. If committing to the instrument, expect 2+ years before confidently performing on stage. Invest in NYXL strings for durability and check your tuner's calibration—even one cent off (441 vs. 440Hz) becomes noticeably apparent. A volume pedal is essential for the lap steel's characteristic sound, and you'll need to learn string bending, positional angling, and the custom tuning system to unlock the full range of expression available on the instrument.
