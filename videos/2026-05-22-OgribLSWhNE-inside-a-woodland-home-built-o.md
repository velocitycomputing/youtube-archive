## Summary

Principal architect Jim Cutler designed this woodland home over a former logging pond approximately 10 years ago, prioritizing integration with nature rather than traditional site development. The design philosophy centers on creating architectural "choreography"—using a deliberately narrow entrance that opens dramatically onto the pond vista to amplify the space's impact. The home features 800-pound counterweighted steel doors providing full access to the pond, regional materials chosen for their inherent properties (Douglas fir for its strength-to-weight ratio, red cedar for rot resistance, and a concrete fireplace that doubles as lateral earthquake bracing). Approximately 95% of building materials come from the region, and the structure functions as a "pavilion" with a continuous roof plane, while a separate guest house requires walking outside to connect occupants continuously with the living ecosystem—birds, bats, frogs, and seasonal rhythms that flourish around the water.

To apply these principles: when designing or renovating, prioritize site opportunities that already exist rather than imposing preconceived plans; create narrow approach paths that intensify appreciation of larger spaces; choose materials based on regional availability and their natural properties rather than aesthetics alone; integrate structural necessities (fireplaces, beams) as design statements; separate programmatic spaces (guest areas, sleeping zones) to encourage intentional movement through and engagement with the surrounding landscape; and design homes as custom "tailoring" for your family's specific anatomy and needs rather than adopting generic layouts.
