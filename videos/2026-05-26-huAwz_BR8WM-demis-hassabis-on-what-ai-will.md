## Summary

Demis Hassabis discussed DeepMind's latest AI breakthroughs, including Gemini's real-world applications (health scan analysis, brainstorming, research assistance) and CO-Scientist, a specialized AI research tool designed to help scientists and mathematicians with hypothesis generation, data analysis, and literature review. The core focus was drug discovery: DeepMind is building a platform of multiple AI models targeting different stages of the drug discovery pipeline beyond AlphaFold, aiming toward curing diseases within 10–20 years through exponential progress similar to AlphaFold's impact. Hassabis explained that breakthrough will require not just discovering compounds but predicting interactions, toxicity, absorption, and side effects, plus eventually automating physical lab verification. He also discussed DeepMind's partnership with EVE Online as a sandbox for testing AI agents in complex economies and player-driven systems, and acknowledged the challenges of recursive self-improvement in physical sciences where verification requires automated labs.

Researchers and scientists can immediately access Gemini and the new CO-Scientist tool to augment their work—Gemini for quick literature summaries and brainstorming, CO-Scientist for structured hypothesis generation and data analysis in your domain. If you work in drug discovery, material science, or physics, understand that AI-designed compounds will need to complete full regulatory and clinical trials to build evidence; early wins through this pipeline will unlock faster approval pathways. For AI developers building automated discovery systems, note the bottleneck: in physical sciences, the validation loop (not hypothesis generation) is often the limiting factor, so focus on automating lab verification rather than just model output. Keep watch for announcements of CO-Scientist applications and the results of DeepMind's automated material science lab, expected within 18–24 months.
