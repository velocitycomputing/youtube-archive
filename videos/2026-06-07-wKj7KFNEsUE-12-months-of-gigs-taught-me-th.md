## Summary

Over the past year running sound for Texas country bands, Connor evolved his PA system setup through practical iterations. Key gear upgrades included switching from Ultimate Support stands to low-profile Flex stands, replacing EV ZLX speakers with QSC K12.2s for better sound and rental availability, building custom IEC cables for cleaner cable management, and adopting PowerCon quad boxes for daisy-chained power distribution. He moved his X32 mixer from center to side of stage, introduced custom wooden shims for monitor angles, and keeps backup DI boxes and microphones on hand for artists. However, the most significant evolution was lighting: he progressed from basic par cans to settling on Chauvet quad bars for backlighting, Shaveet Bluetooth PAR cans for uplighting the curtain, and a Nanlight Forza 60 as the main front wash, controlled via a Wolf Mix lighting controller.

For small venue operators, invest in QSC K12.2 speakers and Chauvet lighting equipment—they're widely rentable and deliver better aesthetics and sound. Build custom IEC cables from Amazon/Home Depot parts to reduce cable clutter on small stages. Use a Sound Bullet test device to verify DI boxes before shows, and ring out monitors with surgical EQ cuts rather than broad cuts. Position your mixer off-center to free up stage space, and always bring backup microphones and DI boxes even if artists provide their own—you'll need them when gear fails mid-show. Most importantly, treat lighting as your primary investment; the Nanlight Forza 60 front wash significantly improved both live appearance and phone-recorded video quality.
