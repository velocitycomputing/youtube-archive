## Summary

Ken Ono, a mathematician and founding mathematician at Axiom Math, discussed his transformation from devastation to hope upon confronting advanced language models. Initially crushed when he couldn't create math problems that ChatGPT would get wrong, he reframed the challenge: rather than competing with AI on factual knowledge, he redefined intelligence as the ability to reason, create new concepts, generate ideas, recognize cross-disciplinary patterns, and develop deep expertise. He wove in his personal journey, revealing how learning about the autodidact mathematician Ramanujan—a two-time college dropout—saved him from dropping out of high school and guided his entire career. He emphasized the critical need to discover and nurture undiscovered talent globally, particularly those without privilege, and critiqued education systems that prioritize test scores and college admissions over genuine curiosity.

The actionable takeaway is to shift your relationship with education, knowledge, and AI. Use language models as a free librarian to explore adjacent subjects without shame, accelerating learning at your own pace while freeing your mind for genuinely creative work. If you're a student or parent, resist the checkbox mentality of high school→college→debt and instead cultivate curiosity and passion about the world's actual problems. If you're an educator, inspire students by showing the beauty of your subject rather than obsessing over grades. Finally, look for and nurture overlooked talent in your sphere—the next breakthrough often comes from someone society initially underestimated.
