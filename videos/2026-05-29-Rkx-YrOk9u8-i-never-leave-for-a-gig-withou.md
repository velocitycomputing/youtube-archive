## Summary

Justin, a Nashville session guitarist with decades of touring experience, covers the essential gig checklist for live performance. Key discussion points include: bringing fewer guitars (two tuned guitars—standard and E-flat—rather than many), using a POG 2 pedal to fake a 12-string instead of carrying one, and relying on one quality amp like a Deluxe Reverb with carefully selected pedals (overdrive, boost, and necessary effects) rather than oversized pedalboards. He emphasizes minimalist gear philosophy, noting that the room's acoustics matter more than subtle tonal differences between similar pedals. He also covers practical considerations like using turbo tuners for quick tuning, wearing dark, practical clothing that allows easy pedal footwork, discreetly using an iPad on a black stand for charts, and packing a Hydro Flask with electrolytes for hydration during long sets.

To prepare for your next gig, pack two full sets of strings (to avoid getting stuck with incomplete sets), bring a metal slide as backup to glass slides, use SGI boxes if running long cables to buffer your signal, and consider a Variac step-down converter if playing in high-voltage venues. Practice slide parts extensively since they're exponentially harder to execute live with distractions. Use charts on an iPad or handwritten in a binder if you haven't fully memorized the setlist—this is professional and expected for one-off gigs with limited rehearsals. Finally, resist the urge to over-engineer your rig; focus on riding your amp's volume knob and staying engaged with the band rather than staring at your feet.
