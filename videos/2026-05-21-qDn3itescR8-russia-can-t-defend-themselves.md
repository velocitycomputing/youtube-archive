## Summary

Ukraine has systematically degraded Russian air defense capabilities, creating gaps that enable daily strikes on high-value targets deep behind enemy lines. The strategy unfolds in three phases: destroying Russian air defense systems (at an unsustainable rate of roughly one per day), exploiting the gaps to launch medium and long-range drones with large payloads at targets 30-200km from the front, and crippling Russian logistics networks. Recent examples include a 200km strike on an FSB command post in Crimea (reportedly 100 casualties) and sustained attacks on the main supply corridor to Crimea, where videos show vehicles burning along highways. Russian military bloggers acknowledge the problem—one noted the land route to Crimea "increasingly resembles the roads in Afghanistan in the 1980s"—but Russia has not deployed defensive countermeasures (observation posts, counter-drone teams, jamming). Russia's response remains a repetitive deep-strike campaign against Ukrainian infrastructure with no coherent strategic shift, relying on the hope that continued pressure will eventually break Ukrainian resistance.

For supporters, the channel is raising funds through two active campaigns: a vehicle outfitting initiative for a Ukrainian drone battalion (€247,000 of €430,000 raised) and delivery of medium-range strike drones to frontline units. The vehicles are critical for transporting troops to and from combat zones, evacuating wounded, resupplying forward positions, and deploying drones—allowing soldiers to avoid foot movement that makes them vulnerable to Russian drone operators. Donation links are available in the video description for those able to contribute.
