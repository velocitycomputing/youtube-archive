## Summary

During his Beijing summit with Xi Jinping, Trump claimed China agreed that Iran cannot have nuclear weapons and would increase US energy purchases, while Xi warned of potential Taiwan clashes if tensions escalate. Trump repeatedly praised Xi as a friend and avoided challenging him publicly on Taiwan, while Mark Logan, former MP and China expert, emphasized that China's core message was "constructive strategic stability" rather than specific breakthroughs. Logan noted no major deals emerged, but the summit reflected a shift in the US-China power dynamic—the two countries are now near-equals, with China holding advantages in manufacturing and economics while the US maintains military superiority. Taiwan remains China's top red line, and Trump's notably softer rhetoric compared to his combative approach with traditional allies like Zelensky suggests he may have made private commitments on Taiwan policy.
