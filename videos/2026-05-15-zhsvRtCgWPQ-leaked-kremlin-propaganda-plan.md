## Summary

The analyst examines Putin's recent statement that the war is coming to an end, arguing it reflects pressure from Russia's struggling economy, military difficulties, and rising domestic discontent rather than genuine openness to peace. A leaked Kremlin propaganda report outlines how Russia would market a compromised settlement to its population: Ukraine remains neutral and demilitarized but keeps some territory, Russia retains the Donbas and portions of occupied oblasts, Zelensky stays in power, and sanctions ease. The report details propaganda strategies targeting specific groups—patriotic propagandists (to be convinced this is a victory), war veterans (to be co-opted into politics), war-fatigued civilians (to be promised normalization), and military-industrial beneficiaries—while messaging that Ukraine will fail within 10-15 years and that Putin defeated Western imperialism.

Putin remains unwilling to end the war unless he can frame any settlement as catastrophically toxic for Ukraine—a calculus unchanged from the past two years. For Western policymakers, the actionable insight is that a competent U.S. administration should attempt to persuade Putin that a deal actually favorable to Ukraine is deeply damaging to it, which represents the only realistic path to negotiations. Understanding Russia's prepared propaganda narratives enables countering the false framing of any potential settlement. Citizens and analysts should disregard Putin's statements as mere image management for domestic legitimacy rather than indicators of policy change.
