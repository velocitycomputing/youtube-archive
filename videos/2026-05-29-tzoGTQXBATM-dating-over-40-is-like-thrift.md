## Summary

Joe DeVito's comedy special covers his experiences as a single 50-year-old man navigating dating in later life, which he compares to shopping at a thrift store—limited selection with unpredictable quality. He discusses why he avoids dinner dates, the dishonesty in online dating profiles (particularly photo manipulation and height misrepresentation), and his failed attempts to date younger women while managing his growing medical issues. The bulk of the special details the physical indignities of aging: he ruptures a vestigial tendon while catching a train, throws out his back putting in eye drops, struggles with hair loss leaving him with a "sides and back" only option, deals with ear and nose hair requiring an electric trimmer, and faces dental complications including tooth crowding, grinding that requires an expensive mouth guard, and acid reflux eroding his teeth. He also describes his parents' aging—his father's erratic driving and his mother shrinking—plus family pressure to settle down and have children.

For anyone over 40, DeVito's observations suggest: avoid expensive dinner dates as a first meeting option (suggest coffee instead), be honest about your age and appearance in dating profiles rather than using old or filtered photos, expect that your body will betray you in unpredictable ways and start preventative health care now, and prepare for your parents to eventually need tech support despite not understanding technology. He implicitly recommends embracing the humor in aging rather than fighting it, and suggests that if you have elderly parents, regularly spend time with them and gently manage their driving habits for everyone's safety.
