## Summary

The discussion centered on how inflammation causes brain fog and cognitive decline. Sugar-sweetened beverages increase inflammatory markers by 100% within a week; high saturated fat without fiber similarly triggers inflammation. After eating high-sugar or high-fat meals without adequate fiber or protein, lipopolysaccharides (bacterial components from the gut) enter the bloodstream, triggering a "postprandial inflammatory response" that clouds cognition and causes fatigue. This effect worsens with large meals and chronic visceral fat accumulation, which continuously produces inflammatory molecules. Omega-3 fatty acids blunt this LPS response, while fasting and ketosis improve mental clarity.

To reduce brain fog, eliminate sugar-sweetened drinks and avoid eating saturated fat in isolation (skip butter in coffee); instead combine fats with fiber or protein (eat meat rather than pure butter). Include omega-3-rich foods with meals to suppress the inflammatory response. Don't eat heavy meals before important cognitive tasks like presentations or podcasts. Replace grazing with distinct meal breaks to limit the postprandial inflammatory spike. Avoid processed foods (high sugar + high saturated fat + no fiber), and do high-intensity exercise to reduce visceral fat and chronic inflammation.
