# Summary

Sam Harris and Haviv Shalev discuss Harris's unwavering support for Israel, which stems not from Jewish identity politics but from his long-standing opposition to jihadism as a fundamental threat to open societies. Harris argues that widespread Western criticism of Israel reflects a deeper moral confusion: young people and progressives apply a simplistic oppressor/oppressed racial framework borrowed from American civil rights history, viewing Israelis as "white oppressors" and Palestinians as "oppressed minorities," which blinds them to the actual ideological commitments of groups like Hamas and the Iranian regime. The conversation then expands to a systemic analysis of how social media algorithms, tribalism, and misinformation have radicalized young Americans across multiple dimensions—polling shows 53% of young people favor Palestinians over Israel, 20% deny the Holocaust, 39% accept political violence, and support for democracy has collapsed to 56% among Gen Z—creating siloed information ecosystems where people never encounter competing facts, only distorted versions of opponents' views.

The actionable takeaway is recognizing that young people's radicalization on Israel reflects a broader civilizational problem: social media has created an "autoimmune disorder" where the information infrastructure corrodes the foundations of democratic discourse itself. For Israel specifically, Harris identifies a critical tactical failure—the loss of the "war of ideas"—and recommends dropping the term "Zionism" entirely in public defense, as it imposes a unique burden of proof on Israel that no other country faces. The larger challenge is acknowledging that countering disinformation requires addressing algorithmic radicalization and ideological framework problems in Western discourse, not just better messaging.
