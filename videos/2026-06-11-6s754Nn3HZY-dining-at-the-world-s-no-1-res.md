## Summary

Noma, Copenhagen's legendary restaurant helmed by chef René Redzepi, is closing permanently at the end of 2024. Founded in 2003, it pioneered New Nordic cuisine and earned the title "world's best restaurant" five times, along with three Michelin stars. The video documents a complete tasting menu from the Game and Forest season (October–December), featuring innovative dishes like reindeer brains omelette, scoby (fermented culture) with crowberry, wild boar with apple, and reindeer tongue—all paired with natural wines from emerging producers. The meal costs approximately €535 per person plus €280 for wine pairing, served in a warm, casual atmosphere with an engaged staff. After closing, Noma will transform into a test kitchen with products sold online, though chef Redzepi's next ventures remain undisclosed.

If you want to dine at Noma before it closes, book immediately—this is your final window in 2024. The restaurant operates Tuesday–Friday for both lunch and dinner with just 40 seats; solo diners can reserve shared tables to meet other guests. Student discounts are available, and the meal typically runs under €2,000 for two people with beverages. This is a rare opportunity to experience a 3-Michelin-star restaurant that fundamentally changed Nordic cuisine and global fine dining.
