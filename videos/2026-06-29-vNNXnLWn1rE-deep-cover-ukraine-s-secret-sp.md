## Summary

Ukrainian partisans operate in occupied territories, conducting intelligence collection rather than traditional kinetic resistance. Ken Harbaugh, a former Navy pilot and journalist, discusses his Atlantic article covering these operations: partisans use encrypted apps, catfish Russian soldiers, observe troop positions, and coordinate with Ukrainian diaspora abroad to verify targets. The Ukrainian military then launches precision strikes with remarkably tight kill chains—one agent reported a 15-minute turnaround from intelligence collection to drone strike. Partisans operate as isolated autonomous cells, often unaware of other agents in their own buildings, facing constant threats from Russian torture, the invasive "Druzya" surveillance app that monitors all phones, and collective punishment campaigns.
