## Summary

The video presents six wealth levels for retirement planning, each with distinct priorities: financially vulnerable (under $100K) focuses on maximizing Social Security and accessing available benefits; stable but limited ($100K–$500K) emphasizes pension elections and tax-aware IRA withdrawals; comfortable middle ($500K–$1.5M) requires a real income plan and long-term care consideration; upper middle class ($1.5M–$5M) demands Roth conversion modeling and IRMAA management; affluent ($5M–$15M) involves advanced tax planning and concentrated position management through strategies like options collars and 351 exchanges; elite ($15M+) focuses on estate tax planning through GRATs, SLATs, dynasty trusts, and wealth transfer. The core insight is that retirement planning is situational—the successful strategy for $250K differs fundamentally from the strategy for $5M.

Identify your wealth level and address its specific priorities: lower-level retirees should verify they're maximizing Social Security and claiming all available benefits; middle-tier retirees should develop a coordinated income plan accounting for tax brackets and long-term care; higher-net-worth individuals should model Roth conversions, manage IRMAA thresholds, and implement tax-loss harvesting; and affluent retirees should engage advanced strategies and estate planning. Regardless of level, ask yourself: Do I have a real (not just savings) income plan? Do I understand my tax exposure? Do I have a long-term care strategy? Is my portfolio aligned with my plan? Is my estate plan current? What am I retiring *to*?
