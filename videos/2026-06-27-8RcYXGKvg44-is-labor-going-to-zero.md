## Summary

**What Was Discussed**

David Shapiro and Ken Yang explored post-labor economics—a future where automation and AI eliminate the need for most human labor, potentially creating 85% unemployment within 20-50 years. They examined how household income sources will shift from wages to transfers (government programs like UBI) and capital income as machines become cheaper and more capable than humans across all four areas of human value: strength, dexterity, cognition, and empathy. The discussion covered historical precedent (farming/primary sector collapsed from 90% to 2% of employment; manufacturing/secondary sector is contracting similarly; services/tertiary sector is now peaking), three-tier wealth fund models (federal sovereign wealth funds, state/province funds, local municipal funds that work like endowments), and why UBI must bridge the 20-50 year gap before capital accumulation is sufficient to replace wages—without it, the economy enters a deflationary death spiral as aggregate demand collapses.

**What's Actionable**

Policymakers should design income security as a portfolio of sources rather than single programs: implement a UBI floor of $500+/month as immediate transition policy (costing ~3% of government spending to reduce poverty), simultaneously build multi-level sovereign wealth funds capitalized by carbon taxes, land value taxes, and resource dividends, and ensure monthly distributions rather than annual payouts for household planning. For individuals: expect permanent career disruption regardless of skill level; prioritize building capital assets (stocks, real estate, business ownership) starting now rather than relying on future employment, since even high-skill jobs (doctors, lawyers, surgeons) face economic obsolescence when machines cost 1/100th as much at 99.999th percentile performance; and advocate locally for municipal wealth funds and dividend policies in your city or region.
