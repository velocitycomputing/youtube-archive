## Summary

A guitar YouTuber discovered an AI-generated channel called "Guitar Gyms with Chase" directly copying his content across multiple dimensions: his old studio setup, clothing style, equipment (iMac, speakers, amps), and even video thumbnails. The AI channel, which has accumulated 5,000+ subscribers and 600,000 views in under a month, produces misleading "rage bait" videos with factually false claims—such as asserting that Gibson and Fender use identical machines to produce guitars that cost $2,000+ more simply by adding different brand names. The creator traced the operation to a content farm likely based in Brazil, and discovered the channel also sells courses falsely marketed as coming from a "real gear guy" with tested recommendations, when the entire channel is AI-generated and unregulated.

The creator is filing a DMCA takedown request with YouTube and reporting the channel; viewers can also report the videos to accelerate removal. The core issue is that AI-generated content operates in a regulatory gray zone where false claims that would expose a human creator to defamation liability go unpunished. If you're affected by similar AI impersonation, report through YouTube's copyright/impersonation tools immediately—the more reports, the faster removal typically occurs. Support authentic creators directly through their official courses or products rather than clicking links from suspicious AI-generated videos with sensationalist thumbnails or claims about "hidden gems" and "exposed secrets."
