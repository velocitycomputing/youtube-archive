## Summary

Emad Mostaque argues that within approximately 800 days, artificial intelligence will render human cognitive labor economically irrelevant. He explains that AI systems—trained on massive computing scales—already achieve superhuman performance in mathematics, coding, and physics, and will soon digitally replicate any job performed "behind a keyboard, video, and mouse" with perfect accuracy. The core issue is that three potential futures exist: (1) digital feudalism, where a few corporations control all advanced AI systems; (2) great fragmentation, where competing nations and companies maintain siloed AI systems; or (3) human symbiosis, where AI is open and accessible. Currently, the trajectory leans toward digital feudalism as companies like OpenAI and Anthropic keep their most advanced models proprietary and inaccessible to the public.

Individuals must immediately begin reframing their identity beyond work, as jobs in accounting, trucking, medicine, and most knowledge work will be displaced within years. Organizations and policymakers need to establish new economic structures and social safety nets before mass job displacement occurs—Mostaque specifically highlights the trucking industry's 1+ million workers who will likely be replaced within five years. The critical actionable path forward is advocating for open, sovereign, and accessible AI systems rather than accepting centralized corporate control; without systemic change toward the "human symbiosis" model, societies risk either dystopian corporate control or fragmented competition that further destabilizes labor markets and human meaning.
