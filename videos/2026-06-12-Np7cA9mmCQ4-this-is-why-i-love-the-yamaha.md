## Summary

The speaker, a touring guitarist, reviews the Yamaha Revstar Standard with P90 pickups, praising its versatility as a one-tool-for-all-jobs road guitar. He details the specs: chambered mahogany body with maple cap, 24-fret Rosewood fingerboard, and dual Alnico-5 P90 pickups paired with a 5-way switch (featuring Yamaha's coil-delay technology for out-of-phase Strat-like tones in positions 2 and 4) and a focus switch that darkens the tone while boosting lows and mids. He demonstrates all five pickup positions across clean, slightly overdriven, and driven tones, highlighting the P90s' ability to combine single-coil spikiness with humbucker fatness and dynamic responsiveness to pick attack.

If you're a gigging or touring musician, audition the Revstar Standard to test whether the P90 neck position delivers the bright tones you need, position 2 provides the stratty funky character for varied setlists, and the focus switch enables quick darkening for songs requiring heavier character—potentially eliminating the need to swap guitars mid-set. The dynamic responsiveness makes it suitable for blues and rock, while the chambered body keeps stage weight manageable during extended performances. Its proven road durability and tonal flexibility across genres make it a strong candidate for minimizing your touring gear load.
