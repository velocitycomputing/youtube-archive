## Summary

Fender sent a cease and desist to PRS over the Silver Sky guitar, claiming it too closely replicates the Stratocaster's body design. The speaker analyzes the design differences in detail: the Silver Sky has a 22-fret neck (versus Fender's 21), a three-aside headstock, custom-engineered pickups, a different scratch plate curve, altered volume knob placement, and sleeker body contours—making it more substantially different from a Strat than it initially appears. The guitar originated when John Mayer, a hugely influential artist, left Fender for PRS; the Silver Sky has become a top-selling S-type guitar on Reverb (which Fender owns). Fender claims they're targeting direct copies while supporting "sufficiently different" designs, and PRS has stated they disagree with Fender's assessment.

PRS could potentially modify the Silver Sky by removing the pickguard or making minor design tweaks to create legal separation from the Stratocaster design—the speaker argues the pickguard is one element that could be eliminated without compromising function. Given that other guitars like the S Classic are closer copies yet smaller companies like LSL (which make ~50-100 guitars yearly) are also targets, design changes may allow production to continue; precedent exists from Gibson's failed lawsuit against PRS. If you're interested in an S-type guitar, the Silver Sky remains among the highest-quality options currently available.
