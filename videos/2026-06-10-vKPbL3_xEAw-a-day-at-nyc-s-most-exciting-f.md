## Summary

Chef Alexa Duchan of Restaurant Lashen in NYC walks through a complete day of French restaurant operations, starting with market shopping at 10 a.m. and ending after evening service. The video covers ingredient procurement (dry-aged French turbot from Brittany, Japanese tuna from Toyosu Market, a 240g white truffle), protein breakdown and portioning for pricing sheets, preparation of complex dishes like pâté en croûte (requiring days of layering and egg-washing) and foie gras terrines with burned grapefruit condiment, and service execution for 85+ guests including Martha Stewart. Throughout, Duchan emphasizes French technique: dry-aging for crispy skin on fish, understanding fat quality and marbling in meat, building sauces around butter, and sourcing extraordinary ingredients weekly while buying locally the rest of the time.

For viewers interested in French cooking, the actionable takeaways are: prioritize ingredient quality and source strategically (visit markets when possible, understand what "good" looks like by appearance and smell), embrace dry-aging and simple preparations that highlight ingredients rather than mask them, master classic sauces like beurre blanc but personalize them with umami touches (seaweed, dashi, condiments like burned citrus), and understand that ambitious dishes like pâté en croûte require multi-day prep work—the craftsmanship is the point. For aspiring restaurant owners, see how early portioning and pricing calculations manage risk on expensive items (turbot costs ~$2,000/delivery) and how menu specials create indulgence and excitement without daily repetition.
