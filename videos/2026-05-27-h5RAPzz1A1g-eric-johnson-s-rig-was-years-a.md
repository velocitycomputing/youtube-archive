## Summary

Josh reacts to Eric Johnson's iconic 1988 Austin City Limits performance, exploring how the guitarist's rig and approach were years ahead of contemporary shredders. Johnson performed unreleased songs from his breakthrough 1990 album "Ah Via Musicom," featuring a three-amp system (Fender Twin Reverbs for clean, Dumble Still String Singer for dirty, late-60s Plexis for lead) routed through an intricate pedalboard including an MXR digital delay system with sample-hold effects that predated modern ambient guitar techniques. Josh emphasizes that Johnson's melodic yet technical approach distinguished him from competitors like Satriani and Vai—his gear obsession (precise pedal placement, selective battery brands, constant volume/pickup tweaking) and refusal to follow hair-metal trends created timeless music that felt "out of place" in 1988, directly influencing Josh's own fuzz-tone and phrasing preferences.

**Actionable for guitarists:** Watch the full performance and Johnson's concurrent gear tutorial video on YouTube to study his three-rig configuration. Experiment with sample-hold or freeze effects on modern delay pedals (widely available now) to recreate Johnson's distinctive lead tones. Rather than memorizing his licks, focus on absorbing his phrasing, chord attacks, and syncopation—the deeper techniques that made his playing distinctive. Test whether Johnson's eccentric gear practices (pedal positioning, cable routing) actually affect your tone quality, and prioritize originality and personal expression over trend-following to create timeless work.
