## Summary

Russian President Putin is relocating air defense systems from across the country to Moscow to protect the government elite, leaving military positions at the front and in occupied territories increasingly vulnerable to Ukrainian strikes. According to Ukrainian President Zelinsky, hundreds of S-400, S-500, and Pantsir air defense launchers have been moved to Moscow alone, while regions including Crimea have been stripped of protection. This has created compounding crises: Russian airfields, logistics hubs, and command posts face higher vulnerability; Ukraine's increasingly effective drone attacks on oil refineries have created fuel shortages across the country, with Moscow's largest refinery offline for at least six months and several regions implementing fuel rationing; and Russian military correspondents report organizational collapse within the army across supply chains, communications, and electronic warfare.

Watch for a sharp increase in Ukrainian drone and missile strikes against Russian military targets in occupied territories—particularly airfields, logistics routes, and fuel convoys in southern regions—as air defense coverage deteriorates in the coming weeks. Monitor Russian fuel shortages as a critical indicator; the situation has escalated from expensive fuel to unavailable fuel in some regions, directly threatening Russia's ability to sustain military operations and maintain basic infrastructure. If this strategy continues, expect Russia's military situation in Ukraine to deteriorate rapidly as front-line forces lose both air protection and logistical support.
