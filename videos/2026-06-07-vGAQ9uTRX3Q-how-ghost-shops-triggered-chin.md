## Summary

**What Was Discussed**

In July 2025, a Beijing customer's complaint about receiving a cake with real (pesticide-laden) roses sparked China's largest food safety investigation. The complaint revealed "Sweet Words Love Letter" was a ghost shop—a fake storefront with forged licenses and no physical location. This uncovered a massive shadow network: 67,000 fraudulent shops, 3.6 million fake orders, and $530 million in fines across seven major platforms (Meituan, JD.com, Pinduoduo, Alibaba, ByteDance, Ele.me). The scandal resulted from brutal competition where platforms spent $14 billion annually in subsidies, incentivizing cost-cutting. All seven platforms had signed formal agreements with a hidden auction app called "Zhuan Dan Bao" that secretly rerouted customer orders to unlicensed, unregulated home-based operators. During the investigation, Pinduoduo employees physically attacked regulators, breaking one official's finger, and attempted to destroy evidence.

**What's Actionable**

When ordering food delivery, understand you may have no idea who actually prepared your food—it could come from unlicensed home kitchens with inadequate refrigeration and dangerous ingredients (a cautionary example: a food influencer's improperly stored tiramisu caused 200+ salmonella poisonings). Verify that restaurants have legitimate business licenses and confirmed addresses before ordering. This isn't just a China problem; brutal market competition driving cost-cutting pressures exists globally, making food safety compromises a systemic risk wherever delivery platforms operate. Be vigilant about which platforms you use and monitor for recalls or safety warnings on your orders.
