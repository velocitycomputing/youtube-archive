## Summary

The instructor teaches "le pump," a jazz guitar rhythm technique that prioritizes steady quarter-note grooves over complex chord knowledge. Using simple three-note shell chord shapes for Autumn Leaves (D minor 7, G7, C major 7, etc.), he demonstrates how to play muted, staccato downstrokes in a consistent pulse while emphasizing beats 2 and 4. The core concept is moving your hand in a constant down-up motion but only striking the strings on the downbeats, using left-hand muting to keep the sound short and rhythmic rather than holding chords open. He emphasizes that solid, clear time and rhythm—providing a strong harmonic and temporal foundation—matters far more than knowing advanced chord voicings, making you someone people actually want to play with.

To apply this, learn three basic shell chord shapes for any song you want to play, then practice playing them as short, muted quarter notes at a slow tempo. Focus on consistency and hand motion rather than speed, and add emphasis on beats 2 and 4 by tapping your foot. Once this rhythmic foundation feels natural, layer in the melody over those chords. This single technique unlocks the ability to play any jazz standard from the real book and creates genuine music instead of sounding like practice, even if you're relatively new to guitar.
