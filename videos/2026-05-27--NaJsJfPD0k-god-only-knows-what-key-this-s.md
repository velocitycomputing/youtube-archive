## Summary

"God Only Knows" by The Beach Boys is an exceptionally complex composition that deliberately avoids settling in a single key, instead floating between E major, A major, and D major through sophisticated harmonic techniques. The song achieves this ambiguity primarily through bass lines that avoid root notes and use chord inversions, creating a suspended, ungrounded feeling throughout. Key structural moments include the intro's clever use of D sharp in the horn melody to establish E major (rather than A major), the chorus's deliberate omission of any D note to keep A major and E major equally plausible, a dramatic mid-song shift in arrangement before transitioning back via an overlapped D/A chord, and layered vocal counterpoint sections transposed up a fourth. Brian Wilson's arrangement choices—from the unusual harpsichord-accordion pairing to the bass-driven harmonic suspensions—demonstrate masterful control of ambiguity and emotional impact.

To apply these techniques, study how the bass line recontextualizes chords by playing non-root notes (e.g., G sharp under E creates first inversion). Experiment with chord progressions that deliberately avoid establishing a clear tonal center, and listen for how Wilson uses harmonic ambiguity to create floating, ethereal feelings. Pay particular attention to the seamless D/A transition between the chorus and final verse—this overlapped shared chord is a practical trick for connecting sections in different keys. Revisit the song with this harmonic analysis in mind to hear the techniques, and consider exploring Wilson's "Good Vibrations" for another example of his compositional mastery.
