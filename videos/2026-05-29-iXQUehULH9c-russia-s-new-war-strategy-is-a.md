## Summary

Russia has fundamentally shifted its war strategy in Ukraine toward massive aerial bombardment of civilian infrastructure, exemplified by a May 24th strike costing $400 million and involving hundreds of drones and missiles. This pivot away from ground warfare reflects Russia's failure on the battlefield: Ukraine has made territorial gains, improved air defenses, cut off Russian supply lines, and inflicted record casualties on Russian forces. Russia's goal is psychological—to demoralize Ukrainian civilians into forcing their government to accept Russia's peace terms—but historical analysis shows this strategy has never succeeded: WWII bombing campaigns against Britain, Germany, and Japan failed to break civilian resolve, and Ukrainian polling reveals 75% reject Russia's terms and 90% won't accept territorial concessions regardless of bombing damage.

The actionable insight is that Russia's desperate escalation signals military defeat, not strength. Ukraine's air defense capabilities improve daily, they're striking Russian missile factories to limit future attacks, and they're developing indigenous weapons systems that will further erode Russia's advantages. Unlike civilian casualties from accidents, Russian bombing hasn't increased Ukrainian willingness to surrender—it's hardened resolve. Ukraine should continue fighting because Russia's air-power-only strategy is historically doomed to fail, and their position will likely improve over time despite current suffering. For international observers, recognizing this pattern matters: Russia is lashing out in desperation while their other strategies collapse, not demonstrating a path to victory.
