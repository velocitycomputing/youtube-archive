## Summary

Russia has enacted a recruitment incentive offering debt forgiveness of approximately $140,000 USD to soldiers signing military contracts, reflecting deepening desperation on multiple fronts. Rather than funding bonuses from the federal budget, Putin is forcing private banks to absorb the cost by preventing them from collecting valid debts—a move that only occurs when government finances are depleted. This policy reveals Russia's severe manpower crisis: Ukraine now eliminates more Russian soldiers monthly than Russia can recruit as replacements, while Ukraine suffers lower casualty rates. Russia cannot increase signing bonuses by printing rubles due to rampant inflation, and is already in a technical banking crisis with over 10% non-performing loans. Putin is strategically spreading the war's financial burden across different sectors (banks, regional governments, corporations) to shield the federal government from blame while destabilizing his own elites.

The actionable takeaway is recognizing this as evidence of Russia's unsustainability despite propaganda claims of strength. Russia's military and economic systems are grinding simultaneously, with Putin manipulating sectors to collapse rather than addressing root causes. Understanding these deception tactics—how Putin deflects responsibility onto banks and regional governments instead of acknowledging federal mismanagement—reveals that Russian claims of strength are theatrical. Supporting Ukraine's continued momentum directly counters Putin's strategy of exhausting international support through misinformation about Russian stability.
