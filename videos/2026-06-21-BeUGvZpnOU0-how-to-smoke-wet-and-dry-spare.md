## Summary

Andy Groneman demonstrates how to smoke St. Louis cut spare ribs using both wet and dry methods. The process begins by removing the membrane, trimming excess fat and bone, then marinating for 2 hours in a pork marinade with pineapple and chipotle flavors. After marinating, the ribs are seasoned generously with Yardbird rub and loaded into a YS1500S smoker for 2 hours to develop initial bark. At the 2-hour mark, the batch is split: wet ribs are wrapped tightly in foil with honey chipotle rub, honey, and extra marinade to braise in their own juices for another 1.5+ hours, while dry ribs are spritzed repeatedly with moisture and rub, remaining unwrapped for an additional 2+ hours. The wet ribs are glazed with fat from the foil before finishing, and both batches are done when the meat shows clean bone pull and tender bite-away texture. Throughout, Andy emphasizes that wrapping accelerates tenderization through braising, while unwrapped ribs develop a more concentrated bark and crust.

To smoke ribs at home: (1) Remove the membrane and trim bones for even cooking, (2) marinate for 2 hours in a balanced sweet-savory marinade, (3) coat generously with rub and let it set while the pit heats, (4) smoke unwrapped for 2 hours to build bark, then (5) choose your finish—wrap in foil with rub and honey for tender, moist ribs ready in another 1.5 hours, or continue spritzing the unwrapped ribs for 2+ more hours to develop a crispy exterior crust. Check doneness by the bone's pull away from meat and the rib's flexibility; a perfect rib bites clean from the bone without pulling all the meat away. Andy's preference is dry ribs for maximum smoke flavor and crust, but both methods yield excellent results.
