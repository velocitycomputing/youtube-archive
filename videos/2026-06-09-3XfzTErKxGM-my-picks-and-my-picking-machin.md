## Summary

Uncle Ben Eller discusses his pick selection and holding technique while on tour with Machine Head. He covers the types of picks he uses—primarily Dunlop Tortex (.88mm) and Gator Grip (.96mm) for rhythm and lead, with occasional heavier picks (3mm+) for solos—and explains why he holds the pick further back (4–5mm exposed) rather than minimally. Using an art school principle of "point of action" versus "point of motion," he demonstrates that holding the pick further from your hand magnifies wrist movements into larger pick motions, allowing small economical hand movements to generate significant power and the rasping tone he prefers.

Experiment with both pick-grip positions to discover which suits you: holding the pick close to the tip for precise motion tracking, or further back to economize hand movement while amplifying pick travel. Try different thicknesses based on context—light picks (.73mm or less) for strumming, medium (0.88–1.14mm) for general playing, and heavy (3mm+) for high-string leads. Prioritize sound in your decision-making, test both approaches, and share your findings in the Patreon community comments.
