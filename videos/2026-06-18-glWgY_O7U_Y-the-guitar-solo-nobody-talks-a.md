## Summary

Ed King, Lynyrd Skynyrd's underrated third guitarist, created a masterclass in melodic, composed soloing through his 16-bar solo in "Am I Losing?" Rather than playing flashy or fiery leads like his bandmates, King outlined chord tones within the G major scale—landing deliberately on D, C, and G chord roots over each chord change. In the solo's first half (slower chord progression), he emphasized individual chord tones; in the second half (faster changes), he switched to the bridge pickup and built a cohesive musical motif centered around D and G, anchored by the repeated keynote D. Throughout, he used the "woman tone" technique (neck pickup with tone knob fully down) to create a creamy, intentional sound that reinforced his architectural approach.

To improve your own soloing, stop relying solely on scale runs and instead learn to emphasize chord tones that correspond to each chord change—map out chord tones in each chord and plan where you'll land over different progressions. Study King's "Am I Losing?" solo as a template and develop musical motifs (repeating note patterns) rather than random runs. Technically, experiment with the "woman tone" by selecting your neck or middle pickup and turning the tone knob all the way down for a different texture.
