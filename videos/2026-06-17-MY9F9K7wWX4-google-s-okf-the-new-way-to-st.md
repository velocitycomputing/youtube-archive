## Summary

Google's Open Knowledge Format is a standardized specification for structuring knowledge in markdown files so agents can understand and interact with it. OKF organizes concepts as individual markdown documents with required metadata (YAML front matter: type, title, description, tags, timestamp), avoiding traditional RAG by instead creating a persistent knowledge graph. Based on Andre Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern, when new information is added, agents extract key concepts and integrate them into existing files rather than creating duplicates, strengthening connections and updating summaries. OKF bundles can be stored in Git, Notion, or Obsidian, and are designed to be authored by people, generated by agents, and exchanged between organizations as purchasable knowledge packages.

Start building an OKF immediately with a small subset of your knowledge to gain practical experience. Use your language model to generate YAML front matter automatically by feeding it the spec.md file. Extract individual concepts from your existing content into separate markdown files rather than converting entire webpages, then cross-link them to build a knowledge graph. Two actionable revenue streams exist: (1) offer OKF structuring as a professional service to businesses—packaging their knowledge into organized concept bundles—and (2) if you have proprietary expertise, sell your OKF bundles directly to others. Reference your OKF bundle in your llms.txt file so agents can discover it, and put Google's OKF documentation into Notebook LM to generate 20 specific use-case ideas for your business.
