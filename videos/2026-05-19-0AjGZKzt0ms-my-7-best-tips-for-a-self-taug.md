## Summary

Thomas Fujita, a professional guitarist and John Mayer's former teacher, responds to a self-taught guitarist with a hand injury history by outlining seven core techniques every self-taught player must master: proper posture (straight back, relaxed shoulders), correct guitar strap use to keep both hands free, controlled picking exercises with a heavy pick, learning triads and fretboard patterns horizontally and vertically, mastering root-third-seventh chord voicings for understanding jazz harmony, and developing shuffle rhythm patterns for blues playing. He emphasizes that self-teaching without structured direction is risky and that proper technique must be established early to prevent the kind of injuries the emailer experienced.

Listeners should immediately establish proper sitting posture and begin daily 5-10 minute picking exercises using the specific horizontal patterns Fujita demonstrates, then focus on learning C major triads across the fretboard and root-third-seventh voicings. Rather than relying solely on free YouTube videos, Fujita recommends investing in either his paid subscription (Guitar Wisdom at $9.99/month) or finding a qualified local teacher to correct bad technique early—a formal teacher is worth the cost because incorrect technique can cause permanent hand damage, as the emailer discovered.
