## Summary

Brandon, a Nashville guitarist, explains why he sold all his amp modelers including a Fractal FM9 and returned to using only real amps. Despite initially being impressed with the modeler's ability to replicate his vintage amps (Fender Deluxe Reverb, Marshall, Vox), he discovered persistent flaws: Kemper units produce noticeable "flubbiness" in the low end, while Fractal units have digital "fizziness," and neither integrates well with pedals or responds to playing dynamics the way real tube amps do. He emphasizes that real speakers create physical air pressure that translates through microphones in ways modelers cannot replicate, and that each real amp—even identical models—sounds unique and inspires different creative choices.

If you're a guitarist deciding between modelers and real amps, prioritize based on your situation: choose modelers if you lack space, budget, or volume capability for real amps—they're a legitimate alternative. If you already own quality tube amps and can crank them to full volume, modelers may waste time on computer tweaking without sonic benefit. Test any modeler with your existing pedal board before committing, as integration varies. Consider whether you want plug-and-play simplicity with hands-on knob adjustments (real amps) or the convenience and portability of digital modeling at the cost of tone compromises and editing overhead.
