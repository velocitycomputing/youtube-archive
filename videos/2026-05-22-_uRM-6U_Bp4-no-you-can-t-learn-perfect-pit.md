## Summary

Perfect pitch is the rare ability (1 in 10,000 people) to instantly identify individual notes without reference, like seeing music in full color—Charlie Puth exemplifies this. The speaker lacks perfect pitch but can identify notes through a combination of relative pitch (hearing which notes are higher or lower) and pitch memorization. A 2015 University of Chicago study showed that adults cannot learn perfect pitch; if your ears don't naturally distinguish certain pitches, training cannot magically create that neural capability, similar to how you cannot teach color vision to someone who is colorblind.

However, you can develop pitch memorization by repeatedly listening to songs until specific notes become locked in your brain through association—the speaker remembers E-flat from "Great Dolphin Street" and F from "Billy's Bounce" because he ingrained them through countless exposures. The actionable takeaway: memorize key pitches from songs you love, then use relative pitch to identify other notes by interval relationships. More importantly, the speaker stresses that understanding harmony and intervallic relationships matters far more practically than pitch identification for musicians.
