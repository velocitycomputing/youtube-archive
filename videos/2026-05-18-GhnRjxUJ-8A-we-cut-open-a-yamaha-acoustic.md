## Summary

Driftwood Guitars, a professional guitar repair shop, conducted a comprehensive teardown review of the Yamaha FG830 ($430). They subjected it to durability tests including switching string gauges, dropping a steel ball on the soundboard, scratching the finish, applying bug spray, and dropping it from a guitar stand. The guitar survived all tests well, with only minor damage (a chipped nut and small headstock fracture from falls). They then cut the guitar in half and found high-quality internal construction: scalloped X-braces and tone bars made of quarter-grain spruce with tight, clean joinery; a solid neck block with a proper dovetail joint; a solid Sitka spruce soundboard; and laminated rosewood/hardwood sides. The thin poly finish, while more prone to scratching than typical poly coats, contributes to the guitar's impressive woody tone and resonance. Final scores: 95/100 and 93/100 (average 94/100).

If you're shopping for a beginner acoustic guitar or an affordable second guitar, the FG830 is a top choice—reviewers found it outperforms other sub-$500 guitars in build quality, playability, and tone. However, purchase a gig bag separately since none is included and the thinner finish needs protection from scratches and dings. If you plan to experiment with heavier gauge strings, expect the action to change noticeably and budget for a professional setup. Even experienced guitarists should consider buying one for casual use (couch guitar, camping trips) without worry about damage, given its durability and value.
