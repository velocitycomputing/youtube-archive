## Summary

The Milk Road AI analysts discuss Trump's strategic trip to China with tech CEOs (Elon Musk, Jensen Huang, Tim Cook) to negotiate chip export approvals, FSD regulatory clearance, supply chain improvements, and tariff reductions—framed as a major catalyst for markets. They debate whether the AI market is in a bubble by comparing it to the 1995–2005 dotcom cycle, concluding they're likely at the 1995–1997 phase with significant growth ahead since pure-play AI companies (Anthropic, OpenAI) haven't yet gone public and current valuations align with earnings growth. The core argument: compute demand will never plateau because agents, humanoids, robotics, and space exploration will perpetually demand more intelligence, making this cycle fundamentally different from past bubbles—and the bottleneck is now shifting from GPUs to memory (Micron, HBM) to energy infrastructure.

The actionable takeaway is to anticipate the energy bottleneck before the market prices it in. Vincent strongly advocates rotating capital from memory/compute plays into solar (via ETFs), off-grid power generation (Bloom Energy), batteries, and power semiconductors (Infineon) because data centers launching next year will face severe power supply shortages in dense U.S. regions—yet the market isn't pricing this risk. Melvin notes the opportunity cost of moving from compute is "too high" right now due to momentum, but acknowledge energy will become critical; the play is to watch for momentum shifts in energy stocks as your entry signal, since timing the rotation before it happens is difficult.
