## Summary

The creator designed and built a supercapacitor-powered wind-up plane using 3D printing and tissue paper wing coverings to achieve impressive flight-to-winding ratios. Starting with a goal to improve upon a previous project that required 2+ minutes of charging, the creator used FEA simulations to refine wing designs, ultimately selecting 10 farad supercapacitors for their optimal energy-to-weight ratio (~3g each). The final 15.6g plane featured a carbon fiber fuselage, 3D-printed structure, a brushed motor, and a custom voltmeter-equipped hand-crank charger that safely charged the capacitors to their 2.7V maximum. The plane successfully flew for 45 seconds using only 4 seconds of charging time.

To build a similar supercapacitor wind-up plane, select 10-farad supercapacitors as they provide the best energy density-to-weight tradeoff for small aircraft. Use 3D printing with tissue paper coverings for wings—this combination creates strong yet lightweight surfaces and enables rapid prototyping (entire planes in under 20 minutes). Design with high aspect ratio wings to reduce induced drag and position thrust below the wing for natural climb characteristics. Keep tissue paper planes dry since moisture absorption in damp conditions significantly weakens the covering; fly in calm, dry weather or indoors. A hand-crank generator with a 0-3V analog voltmeter allows safe charging in seconds without risking capacitor damage.
