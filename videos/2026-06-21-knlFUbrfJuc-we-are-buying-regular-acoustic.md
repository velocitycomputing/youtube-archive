## Summary

The Yamaha TransAcoustic (TAS1C) is a $1,399 acoustic guitar featuring built-in electronics that make the guitar body itself act as a speaker using two actuators. It has a solid Sitka spruce top with laminated mahogany back and sides, and includes built-in effects (reverb, delay, and chorus) controllable via an app or physical tap-tempo controls. The guitar features Bluetooth connectivity to play and record backing tracks, comes with a rechargeable internal battery and magnetic charging dock, a deluxe gig bag, and weighs 5 lbs 10 oz. Alternative versions exist with a looper instead of Bluetooth, and while the overall build quality is excellent, the acoustic projection is quieter than traditional acoustics due to the internal electronics.

For potential buyers, decide whether you want the looper or Bluetooth version based on your performance needs. This guitar is ideal if you want an all-in-one system without carrying external cables, amps, or equipment—perfect for bedroom practice, coffee shop performances, or street busking with backing tracks and effects. However, if you prefer maximum volume projection or want to save money, you could buy a traditional acoustic and add external effects/amp/looper separately; the TransAcoustic's value proposition is convenience and integration, not sound volume.
