## Summary

Ukraine is executing a three-phase 2026 strategy: destroy Russian air defenses, use resulting coverage gaps to conduct deep drone strikes on logistics 100+ km from the front, and conduct localized counterattacks to retake territory. This campaign is visibly working in West Zaporizhzhia (Stepnohirsk area) where Ukrainian forces are recapturing everything Russia gained in the past year, while simultaneously destroying supply convoys across southern Russia and Crimea at a rate exceeding 100 vehicles per week. Russian military analysts are panicking—Rybar warns of potential Ukrainian breakthrough if logistics collapse, while milbloggers report waves of attacks overwhelming their forces and call urgently for reinforcements and drone countermeasures.

Russia has not yet deployed countermeasures despite the crisis, unlike its 2022-2023 adaptation to HIMARS where it found new tactics after months of losses. The window for Russian stabilization is narrowing; if drone strikes on supply routes continue unchecked, the logistics chain sustaining southern operations could face paralysis by autumn, making continued military operations unsustainable. For those supporting Ukraine, the analyst highlighted a tax-deductible fundraiser with Ukraine Aid Operations providing unmanned ground vehicles to front-line units (rewards from $200-$4,000).
