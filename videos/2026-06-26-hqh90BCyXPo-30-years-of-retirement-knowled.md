## Summary

The speaker shares five critical retirement insights from 30 years of advising clients. Standard retirement calculators dramatically overestimate what you need because they assume flat spending throughout retirement, when actually spending follows a "spending smile"—higher in your active 60s, declining in your 70s, and lower in your 80s. Delaying Social Security from age 62 to 70 can add $100,000–$200,000+ in lifetime guaranteed income due to the 8% annual growth, with one couple gaining nearly $200,000 in lifetime income from a single timing adjustment. Through strategic sequencing of income sources (IRA, Roth, taxable brokerage, Social Security), retirees can legally pay as little as 6–8% in federal taxes versus the 25–40% workers pay. The order you withdraw from accounts matters enormously—draining brokerage first and letting IRAs grow creates a "tax bomb" at Required Minimum Distribution age (73–75) when you're forced to take large taxable distributions and lose tax flexibility. The final insight transcends finances: the real retirement risk isn't running out of money, it's running out of time by endlessly delaying in search of perfect conditions.

Immediately restructure your retirement mental model from flat spending to a curve reflecting your actual lifestyle; run specific numbers on Social Security claiming age based on your assets and health rather than claiming automatically at 62; meet with a financial advisor to coordinate withdrawals across accounts to minimize lifetime taxes rather than just this year's bill; prioritize early retirement IRA withdrawals and strategic Roth conversions to reduce future RMD burden; and most critically, stop waiting for perfect certainty—once you've tested your plan against market crashes, healthcare costs, and longevity, move forward and actually live the retirement you've funded, because waiting for clarity costs you the life experiences that money is meant to enable.
