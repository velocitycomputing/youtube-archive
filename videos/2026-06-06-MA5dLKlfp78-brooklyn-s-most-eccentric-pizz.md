## Summary

Nino Kadigio, an eccentric pizza master, opened Lucky Charlie's in Brooklyn, operating out of the oldest coal oven in New York City—a massive 1890 oven that's 17 by 14 feet with multiple heat zones. Working 18-19 hour days while living in a back apartment, Nino hand-stretches dough that ferments for 72 hours, makes Sicilian pasta dishes like anali and cannoli entirely from scratch, and personally inspects every pizza to ensure the bottom achieves "bella bruciata" (beautifully burnt). He rotates pizzas through different temperature zones (600-700°F) to achieve both crispiness and char, uses techniques like panko breadcrumbs to keep crusts crispy, and constantly maintains the coal oven, even vacuuming it to remove flour and coal dust. The restaurant serves only three pizza options: classic, red (with anchovy), and white (ricotta-based), allowing customization with up to three toppings.

To eat at Lucky Charlie's, you must visit in person—there's no delivery or DoorDash, only limited seating. The simple menu (three pizzas plus hand-made pasta) and no-shortcuts approach mean the experience is authentic and labor-intensive rather than convenient. If you want traditional Sicilian-inspired, coal-fired pizza made by a competitive pizza champion who personally oversees every plate, go to Lucky Charlie's in Brooklyn; expect to taste the difference that 72-hour fermentation, hand-rolled dough, and obsessive temperature control make.
