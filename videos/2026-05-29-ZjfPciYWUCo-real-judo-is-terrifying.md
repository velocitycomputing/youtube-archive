## Summary

Olympic judo champion Satoshi Ishii demonstrates his most effective throws—O Soto Gari (outside sweep), O Uchi Gari (inside sweep), Tai Otoshi, and foot sweeps—while teaching the critical technical principles behind them. He emphasizes that 70% of judo depends on grip fighting and proper grip control; without it, throws cannot execute effectively. The video shows multiple training exchanges where Satoshi explains body mechanics (hip positioning, arch control, low center of gravity), how to adjust techniques for different opponents (using underhook and shoulder control against those without a gi), and how to set up multiple throws from the same position to confuse opponents. Satoshi also shares his competitive philosophy: the relentless pursuit of improvement, the necessity of a coach or mentor to guide training and enforce rest, and the respectful mindset of thinking of your opponent first rather than celebrating victory over them.

For anyone interested in learning judo: invest in a proper judo gi rather than using a karate gi, since the garment itself becomes part of the technique and defense. Find a qualified coach or mentor rather than attempting to teach yourself—even Olympic champions credit their success to guidance that forces them to rest and follow systematic training. Adopt a consistency-first mentality by committing to a structured training system and following it regardless of motivation levels. Focus on grip fighting as your foundational skill before expecting throws to work effectively. Finally, maintain humility by approaching training with the mindset that you're never good enough and there's always more to learn and improve upon.
