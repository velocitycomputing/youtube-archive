## Summary

Ukraine has effectively besieged Crimea by systematically cutting off all three supply routes: attacking the Kerch Strait Bridge repeatedly, destroying ferries Russia used as alternatives, and using mid-range drone strikes to devastate the northern highways so severely that fuel trucks and military equipment are being destroyed at scale. Simultaneously, Ukraine has been degrading Crimea's defensive capabilities by striking air defense systems and oil storage tanks, leaving the peninsula increasingly isolated. The strategic value of Crimea, however, is primarily psychological rather than military—it represents Putin's signature propaganda victory from 2014 and holds sentimental value for Russian civilians as a vacation destination, making its loss or siege a catastrophic blow to Russian morale and Putin's legacy rather than just a territorial setback.

Ukraine's apparent strategy is to weaponize this psychological vulnerability during a moment when Russian society is already experiencing growing discontent with the war, government crackdowns on information, and public criticism from military bloggers and elites. By maintaining pressure on Crimea through sustained supply cuts and drone strikes that generate visible headlines in Russia, Ukraine may be hoping to trigger either a mass exodus of Russian civilians fleeing back across the Kerch Bridge—an event too large to hide that would demonstrate the war's failure—or domestic political instability within Russia that could force negotiations or regime change. If you're following this conflict, the actionable takeaway is recognizing that this is fundamentally an information war; consuming and sharing accurate reporting about these developments counters Russian propaganda narratives and supports the visibility Ukraine needs for this strategy to succeed.
