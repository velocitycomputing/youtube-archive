## Summary

Gordon Ryan dominated multiple-time ADCC champion Yuri Simoes 21-0 at ADCC 2024 using a systematic strategy. He spent the first 10 minutes in a "camping" position in half guard, applying heavy pressure to tire Yuri out while preventing him from creating offense. Gordon then methodically advanced through pass attempts using arm isolation (center line shifts with head movement to walk the arm over the shoulder, relying on body positioning rather than strength to control). Once the 10-minute scoring window opened, Gordon rapidly scored 12 points through repeated mount passes from quarter guard, then continued scoring with additional mount entries and submission threats (Kimura, triangle). Yuri had one brief moment on top with a near-pass but Gordon used defensive frames, forearm pressure, and knee-shield positioning to recover. The match ended with Gordon winning 21-0 despite fighting with known physical limitations.

For your own grappling: apply sustained body weight pressure in half guard to exhaust your opponent, moving methodically rather than explosively. When isolating an opponent's arm, use center line head shifts combined with arm extension—not finger walking alone—to move their elbow over the shoulder line, then use your body weight leaning forward to maintain control rather than muscular strength. When defending a body lock pass, keep your knee pointing upward to block entry. On offense, if your opponent's knee isn't flush to the mat, use a "false" knee lever first to plant it there, then execute the real knee lever for off-balance. Time your point-scoring attempts to begin after the scoring window opens rather than wasting early energy on submissions.
