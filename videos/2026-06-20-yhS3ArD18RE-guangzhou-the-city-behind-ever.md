## Summary

Guangzhou is a global trade powerhouse of 18 million people in Southern China where the Canton Fair twice-yearly hosts worldwide buyers, and massive wholesale markets—Baima (clothing), Kimbo (counterfeit goods), and Little Africa (African traders)—supply products globally. The city blends modern infrastructure (metro, Canton Tower, tech campuses like Tencent) with centuries-old Cantonese traditions visible in temples, colonial architecture on Shamian Island, and world-renowned cuisine. It's currently expanding dramatically with the Guangzhou International Financial City development adding 200,000 jobs and becoming a fintech hub.

If you source consumer goods (clothing, furniture, solar panels, cars), visit Guangzhou's wholesale markets to negotiate directly with factories—costs are significantly lower than Dubai or Turkey. For African traders, Little Africa offers efficient access to suppliers and goods selling well on the continent. If you're considering relocation, Guangzhou offers lower living costs than Shenzhen with comparable salaries. For diaspora communities, it's worth visiting to reconnect with Cantonese culture and family roots while experiencing modern China firsthand.
