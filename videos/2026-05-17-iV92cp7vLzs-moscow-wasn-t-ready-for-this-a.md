## Summary

Ukraine executed its largest drone assault of the war on Moscow after systematically degrading Russian air defenses over 12-18 months, destroying air defense systems faster than Russia could replace them. In a single 24-hour period, Russia claimed to shoot down 1,054 drones—averaging 44 per hour—yet many still penetrated Moscow's most heavily protected airspace. Ukraine struck high-value military-industrial targets including the Angstrom semiconductor plant, the Moscow oil refinery, and multiple oil pumping stations, while simultaneously conducting 186 strike operations across occupied Crimea, Donetsk, and Zaporizhzhia. This marks a phase shift: Ukraine has transitioned from sporadic penetrations to coordinated, multi-region offensive operations that overwhelmed Russian defenses across the entire country.

The immediate impact undermines Putin's social contract with Moscow's elite, who believed they could remain insulated from the war if politically silent. Citizens now witness drones and cruise missiles flying in daylight and see oil refineries burning nearby, making official Russian denials about "intercepting everything" increasingly unbelievable. For Ukraine supporters, two active fundraisers need support: Zegrahm Paw's lightning drone campaign (50,000 USD raised in weeks) and Car for Ukraine's half-million-euro vehicle goal (currently 240,000 EUR raised, with 100% of merchandise profits going directly to combat units).
