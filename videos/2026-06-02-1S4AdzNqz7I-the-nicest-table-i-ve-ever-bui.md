# Summary: The Nicest Table I've Ever Built

Woodworker Chris built a custom 96"×40" dining table for a Las Vegas client (Vinnie) with a $20,000 budget and June deadline. The project involved sourcing a premium $5,300 slab from GL Veneer, designing a four-legged base pushed to corners with mitered joints to accommodate 10 seats, and working through technical challenges including an epoxy sealing mistake that cost $300 in rework, professional flattening at two specialized facilities (Pac-Man and Woodwiz machines), and careful assembly with strategic clamping. Chris emphasized that the higher-priced slab was a better value than cheaper alternatives because it required less problem-solving and rework—roughly 20 extra hours of labor and layout creativity would be needed otherwise.

Chris stressed that seemingly minor decisions can have major consequences, using his wood finish business (Sample 73) as a cautionary tale: choosing cheaper plastic bottles that leaked cost him $30,000 in losses and forced rebottling thousands of units. For anyone building custom furniture or designing plans for sale, he recommends: source materials from reputable suppliers, design for easy dimension modification so customers can adapt builds, use professional flattening services for quality and time savings, thoroughly test new tools on scrap before expensive projects, and treat small material and design choices as potentially high-impact decisions.
